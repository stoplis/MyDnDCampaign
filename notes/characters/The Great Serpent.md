# The Great Serpent

A vast constrictor — old and territorial, its scales the colour of dead leaves, its head the size of a workbench. It was the prize catch of the Grand Coach Company's poaching operation in the Hundred Acre Wood: proof that the weasels were not merely taking small, easily caged animals. The dead weasel at the edge of the broken-cage clearing is its work. His expression is surprised. He did not see it coming.

It escaped when the weasels abandoned camp in a hurry. It has been hiding in the deeper Wood since the chaos — not hunting, not circling, simply existing in the new place it has been dropped into, waiting for the loud things to stop. It is not evil. It is not malicious. It is an enormous animal that has been caged, terrified, and let loose somewhere unfamiliar, and it is in no mood for company.

The party encounters it between the cage clearing and the Hundred Acre Wood. It does not come looking for them. They find it.

> [!note] Pinocchio's Account
> *"I heard it before I saw it — a slow, crushing sound, as if the whole forest were being rearranged, and then nothing, and then the sound again, closer. And then I saw the head: enormous, flat, the colour of dead leaves, a tongue that tested the air and found me there. It was bigger than anything I had ever imagined a living thing could be."*

## Tactics

The Great Serpent leads with Bite to deal immediate damage and impose poison, then moves to Constrict a single isolated target. It focuses on the grappled creature rather than spreading its attention — its goal is to subdue a threat, not destroy a group. Legendary Resistance is reserved for effects that would end the Constrict grapple or force movement.

**De-escalation:** A player who explicitly attempts to calm the serpent — and connects its behaviour to the cage evidence ("it escaped — it's scared, not hunting us") — may attempt a DC 15 Animal Handling check, reduced to DC 12 with the explicit reasoning. On success, the serpent retreats into the deep Wood without further combat. Coiled in the hollow it was using as shelter: a small cache of items left behind by the fleeing weasels.

> [!tip] GM Note — What This Scene Does
> The Great Serpent is not a monster placed to slow the party. It is evidence. The size of it — the fact that the weasels caught this and caged it and tried to transport it — tells the players that the operation they are following is larger and more organised than a couple of animal-catchers. Someone is collecting things of genuine scale and danger. That fact should land alongside the fight.
>
> Reward the de-escalation. The party who calms the serpent and lets it go gets the same XP as one who kills it, plus the hidden cache, plus advantage on the first encounter roll of Chapter 3. The Wood remembers when something dangerous was shown mercy.
