# Dancer Puppet

A medium automaton built for the centre of Mangiafuoco's stage — tall, designed to be beautiful, made for the spotlight. Fire damage has melted one side of its face and warped the mechanism in its left shoulder, giving its movement a listing, lurching quality that is somehow worse than if it were symmetrical. It still moves fast. It still spins.

The Dancer Puppet is the most mobile enemy in the theatre. It does not walk from place to place — it arcs through the space in wide loops, balletic and wrong, changing direction without slowing, using its spinning movement as both locomotion and weapon. The partially melted side means it tilts as it turns. The tilt does not slow it.

## Tactics

The Dancer Puppet uses Spinning Strike to catch multiple adjacent targets in a single attack — positioning itself in the centre of a group before striking is worth a full movement action to achieve. It repositions constantly, never holding still long enough for a player to establish reliable range advantage. Evasion means area effects catch it only on a failed Dexterity save, making it frustrating to pin with spells.

The Bar is its natural terrain — the wide loops suit the long room, and the warped floor is difficult terrain for players but not the Puppet, which moves with mechanical indifference to surface quality. Players who force it into a corner or a narrow space where spinning is architecturally impossible have a significant advantage.
