#  Fiamma Ferraro

> [!example] At a Glance
> The flower seller stationed near the south entry of the [[The Town Square]], who considers shared observation a civic duty. She saw the coin fall, she saw the Fox and Cat appear, and she watched all three walk east together — and she will tell you all of it, and then the surrounding context, and then her opinion on the matter, whether you asked for that last part or not.

## Appearance
Stout, sharp-eyed, permanently stationed behind a flower cart that is always slightly more colourful than its surroundings. She has the look of someone who has spent thirty years watching a square and has formed opinions about most of what she's seen. Her hands are quick and her voice carries.

## Personality
Fiamma is forthcoming, opinionated, and constitutionally incapable of leaving details out. She does not gossip out of malice — she genuinely believes that shared observation makes a community safer, and she is not wrong. She noticed the Fox's cane, the Cat's silence, the way the Fox crouched to [[Pinocchio]]'s level before he knew the boy's name. *"The fox was very charming — the sort that makes your purse feel lighter just from standing nearby."* She has excellent instincts for the theatrical performance of honesty.

That said, she does not want to be seen as a gossip or a busybody. If asked to share what she saw, she needs to feel that telling it serves justice rather than idle curiosity.

## Background
A fixture of the [[The Town Square]] for decades. She knows faces, she knows patterns, and she noticed the Fox and Cat as a professional concern the moment they appeared — they were dressed too well and looking too carefully. She watched [[Pinocchio]] drop the coin, watched the two strangers step forward, and watched them walk east together. She stayed at her stall because she had flowers to sell, but she remembered everything.

## Role in the Campaign
Fiamma is one of the three square witnesses who confirm the Fox and Cat's involvement and the direction of travel. She is the most forthcoming — players who talk to her will get the most complete picture of what happened, including the detail that the Fox knew Pinocchio's name before being told it. She requires a small social step to unlock, then gives everything freely.

> [!tip] GM Note — Social Layer
> Fiamma doesn't want to be seen as a gossip or busybody. She'll hold back at first — not from hostility, but from a sense of dignity. To get her full account, the players need one of the following:
> - **DC 10 Persuasion** — frame it as "you're a witness to something important," not "you saw something juicy"
> - **Buy flowers** from her (costs 1–2 copper; she softens immediately)
> - **Frame it as serving justice** — tell her the boy is in danger and you're trying to help him. She responds to purpose.
>
> Once she opens up, she gives everything and more. No further checks needed.

## Connections

- [[Postwoman|Delia Conti]] — passed through at the relevant time; she confirms her account independently
- [[Pinocchio]] — she felt sorry for him; he looked so trusting
- [[The Fox]] — she recognised him as trouble immediately and watched the whole thing
