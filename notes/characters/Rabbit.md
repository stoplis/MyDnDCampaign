# Rabbit

> [!example] At a Glance
> A rabbit in a brown coat who arrived at the [[The Red Crawfish Inn]] for lunch at exactly the wrong time and stayed for the rest of the day because in good conscience he could not leave. He witnessed [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] run their scheme from a nearby table, documented it thoroughly, and has been waiting in the corner ever since for someone competent to arrive. He has a paper covered in very small, very neat handwriting. He is going east anyway, and his road takes him past the Field of Miracles.

## Appearance
Brown coat, slightly rumpled from travel. Upright ears, a small precise face, and the general bearing of someone who has been managing things for years and expects this to continue. He carries a pencil. The piece of paper in his coat pocket is covered in very small, very neat handwriting and is more useful than anything else gathered in Chapter 1.

## Personality
Efficient, opinionated, and perpetually exasperated. Rabbit cares deeply about his friends but expresses this almost exclusively through irritation at their failings and practical assistance with their problems. He is at his best in a crisis, because crisis rewards the skills he actually has: observation, documentation, and the ability to identify what needs to be done and then do it without making a fuss. He will not admit that [[Pinocchio]]'s situation is emotionally affecting him. His sympathy is expressed sideways — via information, practicality, a reluctance to let something unjust stand.

## Background
A resident of the Hundred Acre Wood who was passing through on his way east, travelling his usual road home. He stopped at the Red Crawfish for a meal and arrived to find [[The Fox]] already mid-scheme at a nearby table. He recognised what was happening immediately — he has, a DC 13 Insight check suggests, encountered the Fox and Cat's methods before, somewhere, and it did not end well — and stayed after they left to observe what happened next. He watched [[Pinocchio]] sit alone and insist, carefully and sincerely, that the Fox had paid. He watched the boy run. He noted which direction.

## Role in the Campaign
Rabbit joins the party for the journey to the Field of Miracles in Chapter 2. He is practically useful — organised, observant, good at spotting operational patterns, constitutionally incapable of letting an inconsistency pass unremarked. His written notes contain the Fox's directions to the Field of Miracles, descriptions of both criminals, an accurate timeline, and the note: *"the boy is innocent — confirm on arrival."* When the trail leads further than he said he'd go, he stops and says so — which is not quite the same as saying he won't continue. He is missed from the Hundred Acre Wood, where [[Pooh]] and the others are wondering where he got to.

## Connections
- [[Pinocchio]] — the mark he watched; the boy he is quietly, practically determined to help
- [[The Fox]] — recognised immediately; documented; will not forget
- [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] — was in his taproom; barely noticed
- [[The Innkeeper's Wife]] — her intervention is the moment that let him speak; he approved
- Hundred Acre Wood companions — [[Winnie-the-Pooh]], [[Piglet]], [[Eeyore]], and others are somewhere behind him
