# Carmela Cerato

> [!example] At a Glance
> The wife of [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio Cerato]] and mother of [[Lampwick]], behind the counter of the family bakery on the south street. Unlike her husband, she has no difficulty expressing how frightened she is — and she will say so, clearly and at length, to anyone who asks or stands still long enough. She is the more useful of the two for gathering information, because she notices everything and is not too proud to say what she noticed.

## Appearance
A trim woman of middle years with quick hands and a permanent slight frown that is worry rather than disapproval. She keeps the shop's accounts and knows exactly how much flour they have, how much they owe, and how long it has been since Lampwick swept the back step. She is wearing her working apron but has forgotten to take it off, which is unusual for her.

## Personality
Carmela is warm, observant, and constitutionally honest. She says what she thinks and expects others to do the same. She is not sharp or unkind — she simply does not see the point of performing calm she does not feel. She is frightened for her son, she says so directly, and she appreciates people who respond to that with actual help rather than reassurance.

## Background
She has lived on the south street for her entire married life and knows every household within three blocks. She watched [[Lampwick]] become obsessed with the Mangiafuoco posters over the last few weeks — she tried to get [[Lampwick's Father|her husband]] to take them down, and he said it would pass. She woke this morning to find Lampwick's bed empty and his good boots gone. She has been behind the counter ever since, waiting, kneading dough she does not need, because her hands need something to do.

She has always been fond of [[Geppetto]] next door — the old carver has made Lampwick puppet after puppet over the years, and each one has been received with the kind of delight that makes giving easy. Geppetto's workshop is as familiar to her as her own shop. Today she is afraid for both households.

## Role in the Campaign
Carmela is the emotional counterpart to her husband's stoicism and the practical source for the Lampwick thread in Chapter 1. She can tell the players specifically what she noticed about Lampwick's behaviour in the preceding weeks, confirm that his boots are missing (a detail [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio]] has not registered), and — if asked gently — admit that she thinks he did not just wander off. She thinks he had a plan.

## Connections
- [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio Cerato]] — her husband; loves him, occasionally exasperated by him
- [[Lampwick]] — her missing son; she knew something was wrong before it happened
- [[Geppetto]] — next-door neighbour and dear friend; she is worried for him too; his puppets fill Lampwick's room
- [[The Bakery]] — her home and livelihood
