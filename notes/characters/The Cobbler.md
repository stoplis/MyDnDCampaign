# Rodolfo Amati

> [!example] At a Glance
> A broad, deliberate man — Rodolfo Amati, cobbler, north side of the [[The Town Square]] for twenty years — who knows every face on it. He is currently sitting behind his counter with a cup of tea going cold, waiting for the police to take a statement that the police have not yet come to take. He is quietly furious in the way of someone who has dealt with a problem, filed the paperwork, and watched nothing happen. He is the most direct pointer to the [[The Red Crawfish Inn]] in the entire Chapter 1 investigation.

## Appearance
Broad and deliberate, with the hands of a man who has spent his life working leather. He moves carefully and speaks the same way. His shop is ordered and practical. He is probably in his late fifties, and he has the patience of someone who has been watching the same square for a very long time.

## Personality
Rodolfo is not given to drama, but he is quietly, continuously furious about [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]]. They have hit his shop before — a pair of shoes walked out while he was in the back — and he filed a complaint that the police have not acted on. He knows who they are by sight, he knows what they do, and he says so as established fact, without emphasis, which is somehow worse than anger. When the players arrive, he tells them what they need — *"You want the Inn. East side of the square. The crawfish sign above the door"* — before they've finished asking.

## Background
A lifelong fixture of the Town Square. He did not see [[Pinocchio]] directly — his view is partially blocked by the awning post — but he heard about the ruckus at the Inn, and he knows the Fox and Cat's pattern well enough to have connected it immediately. His complaint is on file with the police. [[The Police Officer]] is aware of it. Nothing has been done.

## Role in the Campaign
Rodolfo is the investigation's most efficient shortcut: a single conversation gives the players the Inn's name and location, confirms the Fox and Cat as known serial criminals rather than opportunists, and routes them directly to the chapter's conclusion. Players who are frustrated by the investigation's incremental pace will find him immediately satisfying.
