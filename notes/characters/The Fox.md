# The Fox

> [!example] At a Glance
> A silver-tongued con-man who operates with [[The Cat]] as his silent partner. He is charming, theatrical, and utterly without conscience — the kind of person who makes evil sound not only reasonable but generous. He approached [[Pinocchio]] in the [[The Town Square]], knew the boy's name before being told it, and had him headed for the [[The Field of Miracles]] within the hour. He paid for nothing.

## Appearance
A sleek, well-dressed fox with a silver-tipped cane and the grooming habits of a man who spends money he doesn't have on things that project wealth. He walks with a slight limp — except when he forgets to. His clothes are good but not quite right: a season out of fashion, or too new for the coat to have been broken in naturally. His smile is very good. His eyes are better.

## Personality
The Fox is a performer who never breaks character. He is warm, self-deprecating, utterly attentive to whoever he's talking to — he listens, he notices, he gives you his complete apparent devotion for as long as it takes. He has excellent instincts for what a person wants to believe, and he offers it to them wrapped in the language of kindness and concern. He is not cruel by preference; cruelty is simply the cost of business, and he has learned not to think about it. He is genuinely charming, which is the worst thing about him.

## Background
A recurring criminal known to the [[The Cobbler's Shop|cobbler]] and others around the [[The Town Square]] — there is a complaint on file with the police. He and [[The Cat]] work a variety of schemes: theft by distraction, the false bill, and the signature con of the Field of Miracles, in which a mark is persuaded to bury their money in exchange for a miraculous return. He knew [[Pinocchio]]'s name before approaching him — suggesting prior research, or a contact who tipped him off. He is not an opportunist. He is a professional.

> [!warning]- GM Notes — His Connection to the Wider Scheme
> The Fox and Cat's appearance in Krat may not be coincidental. Someone tipped them off that a puppet-boy carrying money would be walking to the bank that morning. The players may eventually ask who. This thread leads somewhere darker — possibly toward the Alchemists or another faction with an interest in Pinocchio's movements.

## Role in the Campaign
The Fox is the first villain the players encounter and the one who sets everything in motion in Chapter 1. He is a recurring presence — never quite caught, always nearby when things go wrong for Pinocchio. His charm makes him useful as a contrast to more overtly monstrous antagonists: here is a creature who does tremendous damage entirely through persuasion.

## Connections
