# Serafina Conti

> [!example] At a Glance
> Warm and quietly watchful, with a register that is never wrong and a chalk-smell that permeates her clothes. She runs [[The Academy]] the way a person runs a thing they have loved for a long time — with attention, with memory, with the particular alertness of someone who knows what her students look like when something is wrong. She is the person who sent word to [[Geppetto]] that [[Pinocchio]] did not arrive at school. She is also — and this is the thread that matters later — specifically worried about [[Lampwick]], not because his absences are a professional inconvenience, but because she knows him, and what she has been watching these past weeks frightens her.

## Appearance
A woman of perhaps fifty-five, with dark hair only beginning to grey and the upright posture of someone who has spent decades at the front of a room. She dresses carefully but not elaborately. Her hands are often chalky. She has the quality of giving you her complete attention — not performing it, but genuinely offering it — which is why children tell her things.

## Personality
Serafina is warm in the precise, unhurried way of someone who has never needed to prove she is approachable. She does not express concern through alarm; she expresses it through facts, through detail, through the quiet weight of observation. She has noticed every small change in every child she has taught. When she says she is worried, the word carries no performance at all. Her worry about [[Lampwick]] is personal: she has watched him move from excitement to fixation to something quieter and more purposeful, and she recognises the difference between a boy being caught up in a dream and a boy preparing to act on one. She should have said something sooner. She knows this.

## Background
She has taught at [[The Academy|the school]] for many years. She knows the households on the south street and the square — which means she knows [[Geppetto]] and the [[Lampwick's Father|Cerato family]] by more than their children's attendance records; she knows them the way a teacher knows a town. She sent word to Geppetto the moment she was certain, not waiting for the day to end. She has watched [[Lampwick]]'s increasing obsession with the Mangiafuoco posters with growing unease, and the word she uses — *"obsessed"* — is specific and chosen carefully. The word *"preparation"* appears when she is pressed: she thinks Lampwick had a plan, and she thinks she saw it forming, and she did not act on what she saw.

Her sister [[Postwoman|Delia Conti]] works the postal route that runs past the school and through the square. The two women grew up together on the south street and still share news when their paths cross on the route.

## Role in the Campaign
Serafina is optional but resonant. She is not needed to reach the Inn, but she adds the Lampwick thread with precision and feeling — specifically naming the Mangiafuoco connection, providing the register entries for both absences, and suggesting that Lampwick's disappearance was voluntary and premeditated. This is essential groundwork for a later chapter when the players find Lampwick at the theatre. A party that has spoken to Serafina will understand Lampwick differently when they find him.

## Connections
- [[Pinocchio]] — absent, no message sent; Serafina sent word to [[Geppetto]]
- [[Lampwick]] — absent; she thinks he planned it; she is afraid she is right
- [[Geppetto]] — communicated the absence; knows him through years of the neighbourhood
- [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio Cerato]] and [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela Cerato]] — Lampwick's parents; she knows them well; she has not yet found the right words to tell them what she suspects
- [[Postwoman|Delia Conti]] — her sister; the postwoman who covers the route past the school
- [[Mangiafuoco]] — the name on the posters Lampwick was fixated on; the connection is clear to her in retrospect
