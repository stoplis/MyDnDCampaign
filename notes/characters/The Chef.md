# Taddeo Moretti

> [!example] At a Glance
> The cook at the [[The Red Crawfish Inn]] — a broad, red-faced man who treats his kitchen as a personal sovereign territory. He did not sign on for a fight in the taproom. He did, however, sign on for a fight in his kitchen, and he has the ladle to prove it. His loyalty to [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] is limited. His loyalty to his stove is absolute.

## Appearance
Large and barrel-chested, with forearms permanently dusted in flour and a face permanently pink from years near a hot stove. He wears his apron like armour. His eyes are small and very intense. His cast-iron ladle is enormous and well-used. He does not speak much during a fight — he acts.

## Personality
Taddeo is hot-tempered not out of cruelty but out of pride: this kitchen is his, it runs his way, and anyone who crosses the threshold without invitation has committed a personal insult that cannot go unanswered. He has no particular loyalty to [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] — he works for the wage and the use of an excellent stove. But his kitchen is another matter entirely. Enter it, and you have declared war on something he cares about far more than his employer.

He is not clever in a fight. He has no tactics beyond *advance and strike*. He does not communicate with allies, does not flank, does not retreat. He simply comes out of the kitchen as fast as his bulk allows and stays angry until something stops him.

## Background
A journeyman cook who arrived at the Red Crawfish three years ago and has never quite left, partly because the stove is excellent and partly because he cannot be bothered to look for something better. He has no criminal record. He has, however, broken a considerable number of implements on the heads of people who interrupted his work, and he does not consider this remarkable. He and [[The Waitress|Rosa]] work the same floor; he does not understand why she hesitates.

## Role in the Campaign
Taddeo appears only if [[The Innkeeper|Lucone]] calls for backup during the Inn encounter. He is physically dangerous but completely non-tactical — he comes out of the kitchen furious and stays furious. His *Kitchen Fury* reaction makes the kitchen doorway a choke point that players will learn to respect quickly. Once the fight resolves — however it resolves — he retreats to his stove and says nothing further about any of it.

> [!tip] GM Note — How to Run Taddeo
> Taddeo is the escalation made flesh. He makes the Inn encounter dangerous but not unwinnable — he hits hard but he has no strategy. Play him as a single-minded force: he moves toward the nearest intruder in his kitchen doorway, he swings, he does not think.
>
> The kitchen doorway is the tactical consideration. Any player who steps through it triggers *Kitchen Fury* as a reaction — so the doorway becomes a line the party learns not to casually cross. This naturally creates interesting positioning in the fight.
>
> **[[The Innkeeper's Wife|Vittoria]]** can call him off just as she can call off Lucone. If she intervenes, Taddeo retreats with the expression of a man who disagrees but has learned not to say so.

## Connections
- [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] — his employer; Taddeo will back him in a fight, once
- [[The Innkeeper's Wife]] — she can call him off; he listens because she has never been wrong about anything kitchen-related
- [[The Waitress|Rosa Ferrini]] — his colleague; he does not understand her hesitation
