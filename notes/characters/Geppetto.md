# Geppetto

> [!example] At a Glance
> A small, stooped old man with woodchip-scarred hands and ink-stained fingers, who seems barely holding himself together. He is Krat's finest craftsman and [[Pinocchio]]'s maker and father. He hired the players to find his missing boy, and everything he has told them is true — except for the part that matters most. His grief is real. His love for his dead son Carlo is real. And his plan to use Pinocchio to bring Carlo back is something he has convinced himself is not monstrous.

## Appearance
Small and slightly stooped, with white hair and the slow, careful movements of a man whose body has been used hard for many decades. His hands are remarkable — scarred, calloused, but extraordinarily precise. He dresses plainly. He smells faintly of sawdust and linseed oil. When he is frightened, he looks very old; when he is working, he looks ageless.

## Personality
Geppetto is a man in whom exceptional intelligence and total self-deception have reached a kind of equilibrium. He is genuinely kind, patient, and curious about people — he will ask after the players' lives with real interest, fix things around his workshop because his hands need occupation, tell stories about Carlo with unguarded love. He is not a villain by temperament. He is a man who loved someone so completely that everyone else became smaller in comparison — and who has been living inside a private rationalisation for so long that it has the texture of truth.

## Background
A master craftsman whose work built much of Krat's reputation. He married, had a son named Carlo, and lost them both — his wife Camille to illness, then Carlo to a slow and cruel wasting sickness that stole his sight before it took his life. After Carlo's death, Geppetto spent years researching what he calls *life-essence* (Ergo in his private notes): the dispersed soul-stuff of the dead that, under the right conditions, can be concentrated and used to restore a specific consciousness. He built [[Pinocchio]] to serve as the vessel.

> [!danger]- Hidden from Players — The Full Truth
> The hollow chamber at [[Pinocchio]]'s chest — which Geppetto calls the "heartstone cavity" — is a reservoir, designed to concentrate the life-essence that the [[The Blue Fairy]]'s miracle placed in Pinocchio. When enough is gathered, Geppetto intends to perform a ritual that extracts it, ending Pinocchio's life and restoring Carlo's. He has convinced himself that Pinocchio would understand and consent if he only knew the full situation. His notes, hidden in the workshop (behind a false panel, in a locked cabinet under the workbench, in the margins of craftsman's journals in a simple cipher), record the theory, the design, and the ritual. One entry, dated years before Pinocchio's creation, reads: *"The vessel must love me, or at least be incapable of fleeing. Loyalty by design is still loyalty."*

## Role in the Campaign
Geppetto is the campaign's central moral question made flesh. He is the person who hires the players, who seems harmless and grieving and entirely trustworthy. He is also the person whose plan will destroy [[Pinocchio]] if it succeeds. The revelation of his intentions — early or late — is the campaign's emotional hinge. What the players do with that knowledge is the campaign.

## Connections
- [[Pinocchio]] — his creation, his vessel, the being he has trained himself not to fully see as a person
- [[The Blue Fairy]] — gave life to Pinocchio; Geppetto recognises what she did and has built his entire scheme around it
- [[Lampwick]]'s parents ([[Lampwick's Father|Emilio Cerato]] and [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela Cerato]]) — neighbours next door; Geppetto's most devoted customers, who have bought his puppets for Lampwick for years
