# Kanga

> [!example] At a Glance
> The Hundred Acre Wood's only adult in any meaningful sense. Warm, capable, calm under pressure, and quietly formidable in the way that people who have spent years protecting something small and precious become formidable. She arrived in the Wood as a stranger and simply got on with things, and everyone came to love her. She was taken by the poachers when they swept through the Wood. [[Roo]] was separated from her in the chaos. She has not been found yet.
>
> When she returns, the Wood will feel like a hearth being lit.

## Appearance
A tall kangaroo, neat and practical in the way of someone who has learned that things go better when you are organised. Her pouch is always carrying something useful. Her expression is warm by default and steady under pressure — the face of someone who has decided, at some point, that composure is the kindest thing she can offer the people around her.

## Personality
Kanga is the Wood's anchor. She arrived as a stranger — the Wood was suspicious of her and [[Roo]] at first, in the way of communities that have never needed to think about outsiders — and she responded by simply being Kanga: patient, practical, quietly capable. The others came around. They always do.

She is a mother first, and her instincts are protective in ways that extend beyond Roo to anyone in her care. She does not panic; she assesses. She is not unkind, but she has standards, and she will tell you clearly and without embarrassment when you have not met them. She is the kind of person who, when deposited in the wrong world by accident, immediately locates the most urgent problem and begins solving it. She will not leave until things are sorted. Her definition of "sorted" is high.

She misses Roo every moment she has been away. She does not say so. It sits in everything she does.

## Background
Kanga and Roo arrived in the Hundred Acre Wood together, as a unit, a year or two before the campaign begins. She keeps a tidy house near the Sandy Pit in the southeast of the Wood, sets a spare plate at every meal, and maintains a medical cabinet that is better stocked than the rest of the Wood combined. She is the reason minor injuries in the Wood are treated promptly and properly.

When the poachers came, she was taken. Roo was not with her at the moment of capture — he had gone to play at the Sandy Pit — and the chaos of the operation meant they were separated before she could find him.

She does not know where Roo is. This is the one thing she is not entirely calm about.

## Role in the Campaign
Kanga is the Chapter 3 rescue and its emotional centre. She was left behind when the weasels raided the Puppet Theatre — not what they had come for, not worth the trouble of moving. She has been chained by the ankle to a workbench in the puppet workshop ever since, alone except for the ghost of eleven children who are no longer there.

She did not waste the time. Within the reach of her chain, she arranged everything she could: bunkbeds straightened, shoes paired and lined up at the foot of each cot, names written clearly on the wall so nothing would be forgotten. She has been keeping a precise mental account of everything she observed — who the weasels were, what they said, where they were going. *"Pleasance Isle, next tide."* She heard it and remembered it because she understood it might matter.

When the party reaches her she gives them everything she knows, clearly and without being asked twice. She does not ask to be freed before she asks about the children.

After her rescue, Kanga becomes the Hundred Acre Wood's base camp keeper — the person who makes the rescued children feel safe between chapters, who maintains the Wood as a functioning safe house, and who sets a spare plate at every meal. Her return is the one that makes the Wood feel not just inhabited again, but *cared for*.

She is not waiting to be rescued. She is busy. The party will need to free her chain before she will agree things are sorted — and even then, the first thing she does is go to the names on the wall and read them all, not aloud, her finger moving from one to the next.

## Connections
- [[Roo]] — her son; the most important person in her world; she is quietly, constantly asking about him
- [[Rabbit]] — she respects his competence and he respects hers; they have an understanding
- [[Piglet]] — she has bandaged Piglet's various small injuries more than once and never made him feel foolish about them
- [[Winnie-the-Pooh]] — she feeds him regularly and has made peace with the honey situation
