# Emilio Cerato

> [!example] At a Glance
> The baker next door to [[Geppetto's Workshop and Home|Geppetto's workshop]], and the father of [[Lampwick]]. A craftsman of modest means and considerable pride, he is quietly devastated by his son's disappearance and expressing it, as fathers of his type tend to, through short answers and unnecessary tasks. He and Geppetto have been neighbours and close friends for twenty years — bound together by proximity, by the small daily warmth of living next door, and by Emilio's long habit of buying puppets from Geppetto for his son. He is [[Geppetto]]'s most devoted customer, and knows the old woodcarver well enough to be worried for both their households today.

## Appearance
A stocky man in his mid-forties with flour-dusted hands and the deliberate, careful movements of someone who works early mornings for a living. He smells of bread and woodsmoke and something warm. His apron has burn-marks from the oven that tell a story. He does not smile easily but when he does it is his son's smile exactly.

## Personality
Emilio is methodical, proud, and bad at asking for help. He expresses worry through action — he has already checked the school, the square, and the road east, and come back with nothing, and is now restocking shelves that do not need restocking. He is not unkind to the players if they come with genuine questions; he is simply a man who does not have the vocabulary for the kind of fear he's feeling, and is doing his best not to show it. He will be more forthcoming with [[Lampwick's Mother|his wife]] present.

## Background
A baker by trade and temperament, Emilio learned his craft from his own father and has run the family shop for two decades. He came up through careful work and honest dealing, and he has always expected the same of [[Lampwick]] — a hope that has not always been rewarded. Over the years he has bought perhaps a dozen of [[Geppetto]]'s puppets for his son — each one a birthday gift or a reward or simply a Tuesday afternoon kindness. Lampwick has grown up surrounded by Geppetto's handiwork, and the two families have grown into each other the way neighbouring households do: slowly, warmly, and without quite noticing it happening.

He knows his son has been excited about the Mangiafuoco posters; he thought it was a passing enthusiasm. He did not think to take the posters down.

## Role in the Campaign
Emilio anchors the second missing-child thread in Chapter 1. He is not a clue-giver in the same way the witnesses in the square are, but he adds emotional weight: here is another parent who sent a child out this morning and got no one back. His worry mirrors [[Geppetto]]'s in a key way — but where Geppetto's secret is terrible, Emilio's is simply ordinary grief.

## Connections
- [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela Cerato]] — his wife; the more forthcoming of the two
- [[Lampwick]] — his missing son; the pride and frustration of his life
- [[Geppetto]] — his neighbour and dearest friend; the old woodcarver has made puppets for Lampwick since the boy could walk
- [[The Bakery]] — his livelihood and home
