# Delia Conti

> [!example] At a Glance
> A postwoman mid-round who passed through the [[The Town Square]] at exactly the right moment, didn't stop, but remembered what she saw — specifically [[The Fox]]: *"Silver-topped cane, well-dressed, feigning a limp. Couldn't tell you what happened to the limp when they reached the corner."* She delivered to the cobbler's shop five minutes later and mentioned it in passing.

## Appearance
Delia Conti is a weathered woman in postal uniform and shorts — the shorts are not a statement, only practicality; she has worn them for years, and the town stopped commenting long ago. She moves with the efficient, unhurried economy of someone with a fixed route and an internal clock that has never been wrong. Her bag is not quite empty. She notices things from the corner of her eye without breaking stride.

## Personality
Delia is helpful but pressed — she has a round to finish, and the afternoon is not getting any longer. She answers questions directly and without embellishment, which makes her a more efficient witness than [[The Flower Seller|Fiamma]] if a slightly less colourful one. She will mention the limp-that-disappeared as a matter of professional interest: she has seen that particular trick before, on her route, from men who think no one pays attention to the woman going past with letters. She does.

She doesn't have much to add beyond what she saw, but she proactively points players toward others who know more — the cobbler, the newspaper seller, the flower seller. She knows they all saw something.

## Background
Part of the [[The Town Square]]'s morning routine. She covers a wide route — out past the east road, back through the square, north to the school street and beyond — which means she sees things the fixed witnesses miss: different angles, different moments, the same faces at different ends of the day. She passed through at the relevant time, saw [[The Fox]], [[The Cat]], and [[Pinocchio]] moving east together, and made her delivery to the [[The Cobbler's Shop|cobbler's]] shortly after. She mentioned what she saw to the cobbler, which is how that thread connects.

Her sister [[The teacher|Serafina Conti]] teaches at the school one street north of the square. The two women grew up together and still share news when the route brings them close.

## Role in the Campaign
Delia is the third witness, providing independent confirmation of the Fox and Cat's appearance and the direction of travel. Her specific detail — the vanishing limp — is the most damning and memorable observation: it tells you everything about who the Fox is. She also serves as a natural signpost, steering the party toward the cobbler, the newspaper seller, and the flower seller if they haven't found them yet.

> [!tip] GM Note — Social Layer
> Delia is straightforwardly helpful — no persuasion needed to get what she knows. The constraint is time: she has a route to finish and won't linger. Keep her answers brief and direct.
>
> She is most useful as a *pointer*. After sharing her own observation, she proactively says: *"If you want more, talk to the cobbler across the square — he's had dealings with those two before. The flower seller near the south gate saw the whole thing. And Nico on the north corner — he heard something I didn't."* She connects the dots for players who haven't yet found the other witnesses.
