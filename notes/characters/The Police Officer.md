# Leopoldo Carini

> [!example] At a Glance
> A familiar figure around the [[The Town Square]] — Officer Leopoldo Carini — who is not a villain but is distinctly unpleasant to deal with: he thinks little boys are troublemakers not worth worrying about, he is more interested in the Fox and Cat as a career case (bigger collar, better for his record) than in finding [[Pinocchio]], and he is visibly irritated by parties of armed strangers who show up and start investigating things he considers his jurisdiction. He answers questions. He wraps every answer in faint contempt.

## Appearance
Uniformed, with the slightly over-pressed look of a man who considers his appearance part of the job. He has a notebook he references more than necessary. He stands with the posture of someone accustomed to other people moving out of his way, which most of them do.

## Personality
Carini is not corrupt, exactly — he is simply a man whose self-interest and his professional interest have aligned into something indistinguishable from indifference to the actual problem. He has a file on [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]]. He knows [[The Cobbler]] filed a complaint. He considers the case of a missing puppet-boy low-priority and the Fox and Cat case higher-priority but also politically complex. He will grudgingly confirm what he knows when asked directly. A DC 13 Insight check suggests he knows more about the Fox and Cat's recent movements than he's letting on, and has chosen not to act on it for reasons he would prefer not to articulate.

## Background
A career officer in Krat's small constabulary. Carini has been aware of the Fox and Cat for some time — there are multiple complaints, including the cobbler's — and has not moved on them. Whether this is incompetence, strategic patience (he wants to build a bigger case), or something more compromising is left ambiguous and worth investigating.

## Role in the Campaign
Carini is the system's face in Chapter 1: the official institution that *should* protect [[Pinocchio]] but doesn't, for reasons that are exactly as petty and self-interested as they appear. He is useful as a source of grudging confirmation — he won't volunteer anything, but direct questions get direct answers — and potentially more interesting if the players investigate his inaction.
