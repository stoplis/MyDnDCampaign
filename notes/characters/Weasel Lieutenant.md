# Weasel Lieutenant

Leads the Grand Coach Company's field enforcement teams — a larger weasel in a better coat, carrying a short sword, with the particular stillness of someone who does not need to raise their voice to be obeyed. Where the Enforcers are hired muscle, the Lieutenant is a professional: organised, tactical, and difficult to surprise. They do not explain themselves to their crew. They do not negotiate. They coordinate.

A Lieutenant always operates with a team of at least three Enforcers. Their real danger is the command structure they maintain: a group of Enforcers without a Lieutenant is a mob. A group with one is something closer to a machine.

## Tactics

The Lieutenant uses Bark Orders to chain additional attacks from Enforcers in the same round, prioritising targets attempting to escape, disrupting casters, or threatening to break the formation. In direct combat, the short sword is precise and reliable. The Lieutenant will disengage and withdraw if their Enforcer team is destroyed — they are valuable enough to the Company that survival matters more than completing a failed operation. A retreating Lieutenant knows they will be replaced; they are calculating, not loyal.

Players who capture a Lieutenant rather than killing them may find the information they carry (operational patterns, delivery routes, the name of their contact) worth the effort.
