# Chorus Puppet

The smallest of Mangiafuoco's automatons — child-sized marionettes with painted porcelain smiles, built for the chorus line of a show that has not run in years. Two and a half feet tall. They move in slow looping paths through whatever room they inhabit, following patterns encoded into their mechanisms decades ago, expressions fixed in permanent delight.

When they sense intruders, the loop breaks. They converge. Not quickly — they are small and their movement is jerky, stiff-limbed, wrong at close range in a way that painted smiles are always wrong at close range — but there are always more of them than expected, and they do not stop.

They are not malicious. They are broken things doing what they were made to do. Fighting them is sad if the players are paying attention.

## Tactics

Chorus Puppets converge on a single target to trigger Swarm Menace. Three or more adjacent to the same creature imposes disadvantage on that creature's saves against being knocked prone — combining with any effect that forces that save (a shove, a spell, difficult terrain) to devastating effect in cramped spaces. They do not differentiate targets strategically. Once triggered, they converge on the nearest creature and do not stop until destroyed.

The motionless Puppet in the corner of the Entrance Hall is a Chorus Puppet. The GM decides when, if ever, it activates.

> [!tip] GM Note — How to Frame These Enemies
> Do not use horror framing or jump-scare rhythms. The uncanny quality of these encounters comes from the wrongness of scale and repetition — small smiling figures that converge and do not stop are more unsettling than anything that lunges. Describe the loop first. Let the players observe it before it breaks. Players who watch quietly may identify the pattern and plan around it. This rewards patience and attention over immediate violence.
