# Weasel Enforcer

Common muscle of the Grand Coach Company's field operations — weasels standing upright at roughly four feet tall, wearing flat caps and heavy dark work coats with a small wagon wheel badge pressed into the lapel. Not clever. Not supposed to be. They work in groups, follow orders, and are hired for their willingness not to ask questions about what they are moving or why.

The party encounters their aftermath first: broken cages in a clearing off the road east, tools abandoned in a hurry, and one dead Enforcer half-hidden under an overturned cage — the victim of the Great Serpent's escape. His expression is surprised. He did not see it coming. In his breast pocket: a work docket stamped with a black wagon wheel and the destination *"Pleasure Island Wharf, paid on receipt."* No names. No signatures. Just cargo.

Their methods are methodical rather than violent: nets, coshes, cages, and numbers. They subdue. They restrain. They deliver. The violence is incidental, not the point.

## Tactics

Weasel Enforcers use Pack Tactics to overwhelm isolated targets and Net Throw to restrain those attempting to flee or cast spells. A group of three or four can subdue most unarmoured targets in two rounds if not disrupted early. They will pull back and reform rather than fight to the last — individual Enforcers begin retreating when outnumbered or when their Lieutenant is incapacitated. Without a Lieutenant present, a group reduced below half will scatter.

> [!tip] GM Note — The Wagon Wheel Badge
> Players who examine a dead or captured Enforcer and succeed on a DC 10 Investigation check notice the badge on the lapel: a small wagon wheel, pressed into the metal of a cheap pin. It matches the stamp on the work docket. This is the first physical instance of the Coachman's mark the party can hold in their hands.
