# Rosa Ferrini

> [!example] At a Glance
> The waitress at the [[The Red Crawfish Inn]] — tired, underpaid, and privately convinced that whatever is happening in the taproom right now is not worth the trouble. She will fight if [[The Innkeeper|Lucone]] orders it. She will stop the moment someone gives her a reason. She doesn't want to be here. Most of the people currently in the taproom don't either, but she's the only one willing to admit it.

## Appearance
A young woman in her mid-twenties with the expression of someone who has spent several years being politely ignored by people who should know better. She carries a serving tray and a wine bottle with the practiced ease of someone who has learned to hold both while navigating a crowded room in poor lighting. She is quick on her feet. Her eyes move to the door before any other part of her does.

## Personality
Rosa is not a fighter. She is an employee who knows the difference between a job she can leave and a job she feels she cannot, and has not yet fully decided which this is. She is privately sympathetic to anyone who is also having a bad day at the Red Crawfish — which, at any given moment, includes most of the people in it. She will follow [[The Innkeeper|Lucone]]'s orders because she cannot afford not to, but her heart is not in it, and a sufficiently decent person can reach her without much effort.

She does not agree with [[The Chef|Taddeo]]'s enthusiasm for violence. She does not say so out loud.

## Background
Rosa came to Krat from a village two days east and has been working the Red Crawfish for eighteen months. She has been planning to leave for sixteen of them. She was in the taproom this morning when [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] ran their scheme. She watched all of it. She said nothing, because speaking up was not her job. She told [[The Innkeeper's Wife|Vittoria]] what she saw afterward, quietly and in full. She is not proud of the silence.

## Role in the Campaign
Rosa enters the fight as Lucone's backup alongside [[The Chef|Taddeo]], but she fights without conviction. She is the encounter's soft exit — a character the party can win over mid-fight with a single kind gesture or a DC 10 Persuasion check, converting a two-on-party combat into a one-on-party fight. If treated well, she becomes a small ally who can answer questions about the morning's events that even Lucone didn't notice — she watched from the floor the whole time.

> [!tip] GM Note — How to Run Rosa
> Rosa starts each turn reluctant. She attacks because she has to, not because she wants to. Describe her attacks as halfhearted — a tray held up more as a shield than as a weapon, a wine bottle raised with no real intention of bringing it down.
>
> **Switching sides:** Any party member can attempt a DC 10 Persuasion check as an action while Rosa can hear them. Success doesn't require anything clever — acknowledging her position, treating her like a person, or simply saying *"you don't have to do this"* is enough. On success, she steps back, sets down the tray, and stops fighting. She will not attack again this encounter.
>
> **Reluctant Fighter** triggers automatically: if any ally (Lucone or Taddeo) falls unconscious, Rosa may Disengage as a free action on her turn. She likely uses it.
>
> Once the fight is over, Rosa is a minor but genuine source of information — she has been watching the inn floor all morning and remembers things others didn't notice.

## Connections
- [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] — her employer; she follows orders; she resents it quietly
- [[The Innkeeper's Wife]] — told her what she saw this morning; trusts her judgment more than Lucone's
- [[The Chef|Taddeo Moretti]] — her colleague; she does not share his relationship with his kitchen
