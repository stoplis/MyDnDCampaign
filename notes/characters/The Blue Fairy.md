# The Blue Fairy

> [!example] At a Glance
> The most powerful and most mysterious figure in the campaign — a being of liminal, death-adjacent nature who gave [[Pinocchio]] life and who may be the only person in the world who sees him completely. She appears first as a mysterious pale girl, hair the colour of lapis lazuli, lying in a small white cottage in the forest. Later she is something larger and more celestial. She is patient, she is not soft, and she keeps count of the second chances she gives. She is also, it appears, diminishing.

> [!warning]- GM Note — Chapter 1 Status
> The Blue Fairy **does not appear in Chapter 1** and will not be encountered until significantly later in the story. In Chapter 1, her name enters the narrative only through [[Geppetto]]'s account: if the players ask how Pinocchio came to life, Geppetto says, haltingly, that a fairy brought him — a fairy with blue hair. He does not say her name. He does not elaborate; he doesn't quite know how to. Players may hear a description of her. They will not meet her yet.
>
> Her identity, her motives, and her role in the larger story should not be confirmed or hinted at in Chapter 1. Do not place her notes, library, or any artefact of hers within player reach this chapter. She is a name spoken once, quietly, and then the conversation moves on.

## Appearance
Pale, with hair the colour of turquoise or lapis lazuli — the colour of the sky at its deepest, or of old grief. Her first appearance is of a dead girl lying on a bier in a woodland cottage, with ravens on the windowsill. Subsequent appearances vary: sometimes a radiant presence, sometimes a fading one. She is always beautiful in the way that liminal things are beautiful — at the threshold between states, caught between what they are and what they are becoming.

## Personality
The Blue Fairy is patient but not unconditional. She tests [[Pinocchio]], allows him to suffer the consequences of his choices, and only intervenes when he has demonstrated genuine growth. She does not save freely or often. She is associated with death and resurrection — she dies, apparently, and returns; she gives life and can withhold it. She knows [[Pinocchio]]'s nature completely: what he is made of, what he is made *for*, what [[Geppetto]] intends. Whether she will help the players prevent it depends on what they ask and how they ask it.

## Background
In the original Collodi text she is the Fairy with Turquoise Hair — appearing first as a dead child, then as an older sister figure, then as a little mother. In the Lies of P lore, her equivalent (Sophia) is a Listener of great power who was imprisoned because she could hear the resonances of the world's soul-stuff. She gave [[Pinocchio]] life — not for Geppetto's sake, or only partly so. That act of giving cost her something, and she cannot give it a second time easily. If the players find her, they will find someone beautiful and fading, who speaks to Pinocchio in a way no one else does, who knows his nature completely and loves him anyway.

> [!danger]- Hidden from Players — What She Knows
> The Blue Fairy knows what [[Geppetto]] intends. She has always known. She gave Pinocchio life not ignorantly but with full knowledge of the man who built him — and she did it anyway, perhaps because she believed Pinocchio could survive the knowledge of his purpose, or because she intended to intervene before the plan reached completion. Whether she still has the power to intervene is the question. Her library ([[The Blue Fairy's Library]]) holds information the players can use, if they can find it and understand it.

## Role in the Campaign
The Blue Fairy is the campaign's moral compass and its most powerful potential ally. She is also the person who has the most information about what Geppetto is doing and why. An encounter with her should feel like meeting someone who has been expecting you — who has been watching all of this — and who is waiting to see what the players will choose to do.

## Connections
- [[Pinocchio]] — gave him life; loves him in the way that costs something
- [[Geppetto]] — knows his plan; her relationship to this knowledge is complex
- [[Owl]] — has been in her library, reading her books, leaving pencil marginalia; she may or may not know
