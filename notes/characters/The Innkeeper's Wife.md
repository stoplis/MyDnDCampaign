# The Innkeeper's Wife

> [!example] At a Glance
> The woman who runs the [[The Red Crawfish Inn]] — not alongside [[Mastro Lucone]] but *behind* him, from the kitchen, managing everything he doesn't notice and stopping everything before it goes too far. She is more reasonable than her husband, she knows it, and she has no particular interest in pretending otherwise. She is the scene's safety valve: the figure who walks through the kitchen door when things have gone far enough, wipes her hands on her apron, and ends it.

## Appearance
Stout, capable-looking, with the perpetually slightly flour-dusted appearance of someone who spends their working life in a kitchen. Her hair is pinned up practically. She moves through the taproom with the ease of someone who owns every inch of it, which she does. Her expression when she looks at her husband mid-confrontation is fond, exasperated, and absolutely non-negotiable.

## Personality
She is the inn's cooler head — direct, practical, and disinclined to let a legitimate grievance become an embarrassment. She does not think Lucone is wrong to be angry; she thinks he is channelling it badly and at the wrong people. Once she intervenes, the conversation changes register: from confrontation to negotiation. She saw the morning's events clearly and will say so without drama once the room is calm.

## Background
She was in the kitchen when [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] ran their scheme, but she watched through the serving hatch — she watches most things from the kitchen. She saw the Fox announce that the bill was settled. She saw [[Pinocchio]] sit alone afterward, confused. She watched the boy insist, carefully and sincerely, that the Fox had paid. She watched him run. She has been waiting all day for someone worth telling this to.

## Role in the Campaign
She is the resolution mechanism for the Inn scene — the figure who de-escalates the violence and makes negotiation possible. More importantly, she is the inn's most reliable witness: her account is clear, sympathetic to Pinocchio, and detailed. She is the last person to see him before he ran east, and she can describe the direction precisely.

## Connections
- [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] — her husband; she keeps him from making things worse
- [[Pinocchio]] — she understood immediately that he was not lying; she is sorry she didn't intervene sooner
- [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] — she remembers them clearly; she can describe the side door they used
