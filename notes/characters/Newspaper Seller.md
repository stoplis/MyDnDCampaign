# Nico Carbone

> [!example] At a Glance
> The newspaper seller who works the northwest corner of the [[The Town Square]] — sharp-faced, wry, and slightly wary of strangers who ask questions. He is one of the three witnesses who saw [[The Fox]] approach [[Pinocchio]] in the square, and he noticed the detail that matters most: the Fox said Pinocchio's name before the boy said it himself.

## Appearance
Sharp-faced and lean, with the slightly ink-stained look of someone who handles newspapers all day. He is probably younger than he appears. He wears a flat cap and watches the square with the habitual alertness of someone whose income depends on foot traffic and mood. He does not smile easily but he is not unfriendly — he is simply careful.

## Personality
Nico is wry, observant, and cautious with his information. He is not unfriendly — he is transactional. He gives information to people who give something first, because that is how his corner of the world works. He recognised a mark being worked the moment he saw the Fox crouch to Pinocchio's level. He is not surprised by what happened; he is merely unsurprised, which is not the same thing. A DC 13 Insight check suggests he has seen this before, possibly personally.

## Background
Northwest corner of the Town Square, every morning. He knows [[The Flower Seller|Fiamma]] in the way fixtures of a square know each other — nodding acquaintances who share an unofficial responsibility for what happens in the space between them. He sold [[Postwoman|Delia Conti]] a paper that morning. He watched [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] lead [[Pinocchio]] east and made a small mental note of it, the way he makes mental notes of most things, not knowing it would matter.

## Role in the Campaign
Nico provides the most significant single detail of the square witnesses: the Fox knew Pinocchio's name before being told it. This implies research, a tip-off, or prior knowledge — a thread that leads somewhere. He also confirms the direction of travel and watched long enough to see them enter the inn together.

> [!tip] GM Note — Social Layer
> Nico won't talk freely to strangers who just walk up and start asking questions. He's not rude about it — he just doesn't see why he should give something for nothing. The fix is simple:
> - **Buy a newspaper** (1 copper, or whatever small coin fits). The moment money changes hands, he relaxes.
> - No Persuasion check needed after that — he answers everything directly and without embellishment.
>
> He is *not* holding back information maliciously. He is simply a man whose corner of the world runs on small transactions, and this is one of them. Once paid, he is a reliable and precise witness.

## Connections
- [[The Flower Seller|Fiamma]] — fellow square witness; they confirm each other's accounts
- [[Postwoman|Delia Conti]] — bought a paper that morning; remembers talking to her about the strangers briefly
- [[The Fox]] — recognised him immediately as trouble and did not act on it, which he is mildly uncomfortable about
- [[Pinocchio]] — the mark; Nico is sorry in a practical way
