# Lampwick

> [!example] At a Glance
> [[Pinocchio]]'s closest friend and worst influence — the baker's son from next door, who has been obsessed for weeks with the idea of running away to join Mangiafuoco's Puppet Theatre. He is brash, irreverent, and absolutely certain he can handle himself. He is also, as of this morning, missing. Unlike Pinocchio, he wasn't taken — he left. He just didn't tell anyone he was going.

## Appearance
A sturdy, energetic boy with ink-stained fingers from school and flour-dust on his clothes from his father's bakery. He has the look of someone who is always about to be somewhere else. He dresses practically, or would if he thought about it; mostly he just dresses quickly. His eyes are bright and his attention span is short, except when he is excited about something — then he is relentless.

## Personality
Lampwick is the kind of boy who makes bad ideas sound reasonable through sheer enthusiasm. He is not malicious — he genuinely believes that the things he wants to do will be fine, because in his experience, things have mostly been fine. He has a talent for ignoring warning signs, a constitutional inability to defer pleasure, and a loyalty to Pinocchio that is genuine even when it leads them both into trouble. He acts first and considers second, if at all.

## Background
The son of [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio Cerato]] and [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela Cerato]], who run the bakery next door to [[Geppetto's Workshop and Home|Geppetto's workshop]]. He and Pinocchio have been friends for as long as Pinocchio has been alive — they share a wall and a school desk. Lately, Lampwick has become fixated on the posters for Mangiafuoco's Puppet Theatre that have been appearing around [[The Town Square]]. The [[The Academy|schoolmaster]] noticed the obsession weeks ago. This morning, instead of going to school, Lampwick followed the posters' promise east. He does not know yet that it leads somewhere he cannot easily leave.

> [!warning]- GM Notes — His Fate
> Lampwick's disappearance is a separate thread from Pinocchio's. He ran away *willingly* and is currently at Mangiafuoco's Theatre — enthusiastic, naive, and increasingly less free. By the time the players reach the theatre in a later chapter, Lampwick will be inside it, technically there by choice and practically unable to leave. His transformation in the book — into a donkey, in the Land of Toys — is the fate that awaits him if the players do not intervene in time. His name, Lucignolo, means "candle wick": something that burns bright and brief.

## Role in the Campaign
Lampwick is the campaign's secondary human thread alongside Pinocchio — a second missing child, a second worried parent, and a second moral question. His story is about how easily willing complicity slides into captivity. The players may not be able to save him. They will know, in retrospect, exactly when they could have.

## Connections
- [[Pinocchio]] — his best friend; they share a wall, a school, and an alarming number of bad decisions
- [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio Cerato]] — his father, worried and too proud to say so
- [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela Cerato]] — his mother, worried and not too proud to say so at all
- [[Geppetto]] — the next-door neighbour who has always been kind to him
- [[The Schoolmaster]] — flagged his obsession with the theatre weeks ago
