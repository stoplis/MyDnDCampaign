# Roo

> [!example] At a Glance
> A very small joey — barely past the size of a large rabbit — with enormous ears and an expression of determined bravery that is mostly instinct rather than thought. He ended up in [[The Field of Miracles]] after being separated from his mother when the poachers came. The Fox had been telling him his mother was coming, if he just stayed very still. He stayed very still for a long time. He is overjoyed to be going home.

## Appearance
Small, young, ears almost too big for his head. Orange-brown fur, bright eyes, a tail that he is still learning to do anything useful with. He moves by bouncing, mostly, even when walking would be more efficient. He is wearing a small knitted thing that Kanga made him.

## Personality
Roo is in love with the world, and the world has not yet given him any good reason to stop. He is excited by everything — including, inadvertently, things he probably should not be excited about. He has no fear in the conventional sense because he has no framework for danger; Kanga has kept it away from him and he has not yet noticed the absence. He is kind without understanding that kindness is a virtue; it simply does not occur to him to be otherwise. He is not naive — he is genuinely, unguardedly *good*, which is a different and more valuable thing.

He is what Pinocchio is trying to become. Both of them are learning what it means to be a real child.

## Background
Roo lives with [[Kanga]] in the southeast of the Hundred Acre Wood, near the Sandy Pit. He was at home when the poachers came — not caught in the cages, but swept out of the Wood in the confusion and chaos. He found his way to [[The Field of Miracles]] clearing and stayed there because he did not know which direction was home, and because the Fox told him to stay still.

He was staying very still when the party found him.

## Role in the Campaign
Roo is the first Pooh character the party brings home — the first milestone, the first level-up, the first sign that bringing people back to the Wood is both possible and worth doing. His return in Chapter 2 is the chapter's emotional close.

He is not a combat asset. He is a reminder of why the Wood matters.

His mother [[Kanga]] was captured by the poachers and will be found in a later chapter.

## Connections
- [[Kanga]] — his mother; currently missing, captured by the poachers; he misses her constantly and speaks of her often
- [[Rabbit]] — Roo admires Rabbit with a completeness that Rabbit finds both touching and slightly exhausting
- [[Winnie-the-Pooh]] — Roo's other great hero; he has memorised several of Pooh's humming-songs
- [[Tigger]] — Roo's model and aspiration; he wants to bounce like Tigger when he is bigger

> [!warning] Important GM Note
> Roo is not a liability in the conventional sense — he is not helpless, and he will not panic. But he *is* small and has no concept of when something is actually dangerous. The party will instinctively want to protect him, and that instinct is correct, and they should not be made to feel foolish for having it. His role in combat is comic and warm, not perilous.
