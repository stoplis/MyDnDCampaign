# Mastro Lucone

> [!example] At a Glance
> The proprietor of the [[The Red Crawfish Inn]] — a broad, barrel-chested man in a permanently stained apron who has been cheated one too many times and has decided the only cure is to stop letting people leave without paying. He is not a villain. He is a working man who was swindled by [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]], had no one to punish for it but [[Pinocchio]], and transferred his legitimate grievance to the wrong target. He is the campaign's first obstacle, and resolving him with grace teaches the players something about how this world works.

## Appearance
Large and broad, with a face made for displeasure and forearms built from thirty years of hauling barrels. His apron has stains that predate this decade. He stands in doorways as though he was designed for blocking them. When angry, his face goes a particular shade of red that his wife reads as an early warning signal.

## Personality
Lucone is not cruel — he is righteously furious in a way that has calcified into policy. He was wronged. He knows exactly who wronged him, and he cannot catch them, so when someone he *can* catch appears in his taproom, the logic of his grievance simply transfers. He is stubborn rather than stupid; he can be reasoned with once the room has settled, but he requires acknowledgement that the wrong done to him was real. The DC drops considerably when the players treat his anger as earned rather than excessive.

## Background
A career innkeeper who has run the Red Crawfish for years and has learned to read a table the way a sailor reads weather. He knew the Fox was running a scheme — *"the sort that makes your purse feel lighter just from standing nearby"* — and should have said something, and didn't, and has been quietly furious at himself about that since. His wife [[The Innkeeper's Wife|Vittoria]] manages the kitchen and manages him; the inn runs because of them both.

## Role in the Campaign
Mastro Lucone is the tutorial encounter's human face. How the players handle him — the combination of fight and negotiation that follows — sets the tone for how they'll approach complex social situations throughout the campaign. He is also, once resolved, a reliable witness and a useful contact who knows who the Fox and Cat are and what they do.

## Connections
- [[The Innkeeper's Wife]] — his wife; the safety valve; she stopped this going too far
- [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] — cheated him; he has a description of both and would love someone to do something about them
- [[Pinocchio]] — the unintended target of his legitimate anger
- [[Rabbit]] — was sitting in his corner through the whole incident; Lucone barely noticed him
- [[The Police Officer]] — has a complaint on file; nothing has happened
