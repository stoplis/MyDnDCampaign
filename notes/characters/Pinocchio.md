# Pinocchio

> [!example] At a Glance
> A puppet boy brought to life by miraculous fairy magic — carved by [[Geppetto]] from an enchanted block of wood and given breath, motion, and something very close to a soul. He is not like other children, though he desperately wants to be. He is curious, warm-hearted, and constitutionally incapable of resisting a persuasive story. His nose grows when he lies. He is currently missing.

## Appearance
A slender, articulate boy carved from pale hardwood, with jointed limbs, painted features, and a shock of dark hair. His joints are visible at the knees, elbows, and wrists if one looks closely. His eyes are unusually bright — the colour of old amber — and his expression is open in a way that makes him an easy target. He wears the clothes Geppetto made him: a small hat, a coat, short trousers. A hollow chamber sits at the centre of his chest, beneath the ribs, which [[Geppetto]] calls the "heartstone cavity."

## Personality
Pinocchio is not bad — he is *unfinished*. He acts on impulse, believes what he is told by those who seem kind, and finds it genuinely difficult to imagine that a smiling face might conceal a predatory intention. He is not stupid, but he has had very little time to be a person, and the world has not been gentle about teaching him. He loves Geppetto completely and without question. That love is the most dangerous thing about him.

## Background
Carved from a block of enchanted wood by the elderly craftsman [[Geppetto]], who had lost his real son Carlo to illness. The [[The Blue Fairy]] granted Pinocchio life at Geppetto's wish — or perhaps at her own inscrutable discretion. He has been alive for a relatively short time and has spent most of it in Geppetto's workshop, helping with small tasks and attending school. He knows he is not an ordinary boy. He does not know the full truth of why he was made.

> [!danger]- Hidden from Players — The Truth of His Making
> Pinocchio was not created purely out of love. He was built by [[Geppetto]] as a vessel: a chamber at his chest is designed to concentrate and hold the life-essence (Ergo) that the fairy's miracle placed in him. Geppetto intends, when sufficient essence is gathered, to extract it and use it to resurrect Carlo — a process that will end Pinocchio. Pinocchio does not know this. He may, at some deep level, sense that something about his father's love is not entirely what it seems.

## Role in the Campaign
Pinocchio is the heart of the campaign — the person the players are sent to find, the moral question they are sent to answer. Is he a real person? Does he deserve to live? What does it mean to become human? Every chapter circles back to him. His choices — what he believes, what he refuses to believe, whether he lies to protect the people he loves — drive the story's emotional core.

## Connections
- [[Geppetto]] — his maker and father figure; the love between them is genuine and the danger is real
- [[The Blue Fairy]] — gave him life; she is the only one who sees him completely
- [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] — exploited his trust in Chapter 1
- [[Lampwick]] — his closest friend and worst influence
- [[Rabbit]] — a recent, unlikely ally who saw what happened to him and chose to help
