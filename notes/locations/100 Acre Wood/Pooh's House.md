# Pooh's House ("Mr Sanders")

> [!info] At a Glance
> A single-room dwelling at the base of a large beech tree, accessed through a low round door. A sign above the door reads MR SANDERS — a name the house had when Pooh moved in, and which Pooh did not choose and does not entirely understand. Inside: one chair, one table, one fireplace, a great many honey jars.

## Description

The house is warm and slightly sticky underfoot near the kitchen area. It smells of beeswax and something sweet. The chair by the fireplace has a permanent Pooh-shaped impression in the cushion. The honey jars line every available shelf; the ones at the back are older and have been there long enough to become part of the furniture. There is a very small door-within-the-door at the bottom for emergencies (the emergencies being: Pooh gets stuck in the main door after visiting Rabbit).

## When Pooh is Gone

The door is ajar. Inside, the honey pots are knocked over — one shattered on the floor. Pooh's chair is pushed back at the wrong angle. On the table, a smackerel half-eaten and a note: *"Gone out. Back soon."* He meant it when he wrote it.

A Passive Perception 14 notices the hidden shelf behind the fireplace brick — Pooh's most special honey jar, kept for emergencies. He has not taken it. This should say something.

## When Pooh is Home

The smell reaches the path before the house does. The chair is occupied. Pooh is probably thinking about something simple with the full force of his considerable heart.

He will offer the party a smackerel. They should accept.
