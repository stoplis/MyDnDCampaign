# Rabbit's Warren and Garden

> [!info] At a Glance
> An extensive burrow with a front door, back door, a side door for guests who overstay, and several windows Rabbit installed himself. The garden outside is immaculate: carrots in precise rows, a compost situation that Rabbit takes personally, a gate with a latch that requires a specific technique.

## Description

The garden is the first sign that someone competent lives here. The rows are exactly even. The compost pile is labelled. The gate latch requires pressing down and then sideways — Rabbit put it this way on purpose; it discourages casual callers. Friends and Relations live in lesser burrows nearby; Rabbit keeps track of them in a separate ledger.

Inside, the burrow is organised by a principle that is entirely Rabbit's own and which visitors rarely grasp immediately. It makes complete sense once explained.

## When Rabbit is Gone

The garden is tidy but unattended — a day or two without weeding is just beginning to show. Inside, a half-eaten supper on the kitchen table. A second pencil on the desk beside an unfinished list. The list should not be read. It is private.

## When Rabbit is Home

The gate latch must be done correctly or Rabbit will hear it and come to the door with an expression. The larder contains a truly extraordinary quantity of vegetables and a very tidy inventory ledger. Players who read the ledger learn that Pooh has been taking honey "on loan" since March and the debt has been accumulating interest.

## The Back Door

The side door "for guests who overstay" is not mythological. It has a latch from the inside and Rabbit uses it when a visit has exceeded its natural length. Pooh has been escorted through it on multiple occasions and seems untroubled by this.
