# Piglet's House — Trespassers W

> [!info] At a Glance
> A small old beech tree with a sign reading TRESPASSERS W (the rest of the name having blown away long ago). The interior is tidy, slightly too tidy — the home of a small creature who has worked hard to make itself feel safe. Pressed flowers in frames. A kettle. A very small armchair.

## Description

The door is 2.5 feet high. Medium or larger creatures must squeeze to enter, which is probably the point. Inside, the house has the quality of somewhere that has been arranged very carefully by someone who needed the arrangement to matter: the flowers pressed and labelled, the kettle on its hook, the single armchair positioned to face both the fireplace and the door at once.

It smells of dried flowers and something faintly sweet.

## When Piglet is Gone

A brave little note is tacked to the door: *"Gone to find help. Back soon. — Piglet."* He left it for anyone who came. The pressed flowers inside are undisturbed. The armchair is empty.

Piglet's emergency hazel-nuts are stored under the floorboards (DC 13 Perception). He uses them in dire situations, which by his definition includes most Wednesdays.

## When Piglet is Home

The kettle is on. Piglet will have heard the party coming from quite far away and will have had time to decide whether they are friendly. If they are, he opens the door fully. If he is uncertain, only the top half.

He will apologise several times for the state of the house, which is immaculate. This is simply how he is.
