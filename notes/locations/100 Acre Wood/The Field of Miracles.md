# The Field of Miracles

> [!info] At a Glance
> A clearing at the eastern edge of the Hundred Acre Wood. Beautiful in a way that is slightly wrong. The light is too golden. The soil is too dark. The silence is more complete than even the empty Wood's. Nothing grows in the patches of earth at the centre of the clearing where coins have been buried.

## Description

The Field existed before the Wood grew up around it. Whether it is older, or simply outside the Wood's particular magic, is unclear. The trees at its edge lean slightly away from it, as if they did not choose to border it but ended up there regardless.

In the original story, the Fox tells Pinocchio that burying gold coins here will yield a hundredfold harvest by morning. This is a lie, of course. It is one of the oldest lies. The name *Campo dei Miracoli* — Field of Miracles — is also the name of the real square in Pisa where the Leaning Tower stands, which is perhaps Collodi's joke: a place of genuine wonder, exploited for a swindle.

Nothing miraculous has ever happened here. The coins stay underground, or are dug up by the Fox and Cat after the mark has gone.

## As a D&D Environment

The clearing is fifty feet across, circular, the earth unusually dark and soft. Combat here is open — no cover, no difficult terrain, nothing to hide behind except the treeline at the edges. This makes the Fox's eventual escape easier; he is already near the trees.

The patches of dead earth in the centre (where coins have been buried) are slightly uneven underfoot — not difficult terrain, but worth a DC 10 Perception check to notice before moving through quickly.

**Chapter 2 encounter space:** This is where the party finds [[The Fox]], [[The Cat]], and [[Roo]] in Chapter 2. See [[Chapter 2 - The Field of Miracles]] for the full encounter.

## What the Clearing Knows

The Field does not get better when the Wood heals. It simply gets quieter. Whatever the Fox and Cat's repeated use of it has done to the ground, that does not undo. The party may notice this later — when all the Pooh characters are home and the Wood is warm and full of life, this clearing remains the same. Slightly too golden. Slightly too still.

This is probably worth a question at some point.
