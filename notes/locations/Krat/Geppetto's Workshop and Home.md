# Geppetto's Workshop and Home

> [!info] Description
> A narrow street south of the [[The Town Square|square]], cobblestones smooth from decades of traffic, a smell of fresh bread drifting from the bakery next door. Geppetto's workshop occupies the ground floor; the family lives above. The door is unlocked and half-open. Inside: sawdust, linseed oil, the cold smell of an unlit brazier. Half-finished puppets hang from hooks along the ceiling. The stool at Geppetto's workbench is pushed back at an angle, as if someone stood up quickly and didn't think to straighten it.
>
> The space where a living child ought to have left marks — scuffs, messes, the evidence of habitation — is oddly sterile. The puppets hang with the patient stillness of things that have never moved. Everything is in its place. The tools are cared for with obsessive attention.

## Characters Here

- [[Geppetto]] — small, white-haired, red-eyed from a night without sleep. The quest-giver and the campaign's central mystery.

## What Players Can Learn

[[Pinocchio]] left at half past seven carrying the week's shop takings (roughly 8 gold pieces) to deposit at the bank, then go on to school. His route: out the door, left onto the south street, right into the square, west to the bank. He did not arrive at the bank. He did not arrive at school.

**The bakery next door** is run by [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio]] and [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela Cerato]] — [[Geppetto]]'s most devoted customers, who have been buying his puppets for [[Lampwick]] for years. Their son — [[Lampwick]], [[Pinocchio]]'s closest friend — is also missing. He left considerably later, was running late, and departed after [[Pinocchio]] had already gone.

**DC 12 Perception:** A single gold coin is wedged between the floorboards near the door — the purse was knocking against the banister all the way down. [[Geppetto]] doesn't notice it.

**For the GM:** [[Geppetto]]'s design drawings for [[Pinocchio]] are visible in the workshop — not hidden. The hollow chamber at the chest is clearly visible. If asked, Geppetto says it is where the *heartstone* sits. Hidden notes (behind a false panel, in a locked cabinet under the workbench, in the margins of craftsman's journals in a simple cipher) tell the true story.

## GM Notes

This is where the campaign begins. Play the space as warm and detailed — clocks, music boxes, toys, half-finished wonders — but let the absence of living-child-mess land. [[Geppetto]] sticks to facts. He says *"my boy"* with tenderness, but notice: he never describes [[Pinocchio]] as a person. He describes qualities the way a craftsman describes a fine piece of work. Players who are paying attention will feel this without being able to name it.

The coin in the floor is a small reward for thorough players. The hollow-chamber drawing is a long-game plant. Let both sit.
