# The Bakery

> [!info] Description
> A ground-floor shopfront immediately next door to [[Geppetto's Workshop and Home]], on the south street. The window displays round loaves, braided rolls, and a tray of biscotti going golden near the glass. Inside: a warm working bench, sacks of flour stacked against the far wall, a large iron oven that has been cold since this morning — not because the baking is done, but because nobody lit it properly today. The bread that was made was made early, before things went wrong. The back step has not been swept.

## Characters Here

- [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio Cerato]] — [[Lampwick]]'s father; at his workbench, restocking shelves that don't need restocking. He answers questions with short sentences and silences that mean more than he intends.
- [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela Cerato]] — [[Lampwick]]'s mother; behind the counter. She will tell the players exactly how frightened she is and expects them to do something useful with that information.

## What Players Can Learn

[[Lampwick]], [[Pinocchio]]'s closest friend, is also missing. He was not taken — his good boots are gone (a detail [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio]] has not yet registered, but [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela]] has). He left early, before his parents were up.

[[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela]] can confirm: [[Lampwick]] has been obsessed with the Mangiafuoco posters for weeks. She tried to get her husband to take them down. He said it would pass. She noticed the boots before he did. She thinks [[Lampwick]] had a plan.

[[Lampwick's Father|Emilio]] is [[Geppetto]]'s most devoted customer — the two old men have been neighbours and friends for twenty years, brought together by Emilio's long habit of buying Geppetto's puppets for his son. He is worried for both households today, though he will not say so in those words.

## GM Notes

The Bakery is where the Lampwick thread begins — the second missing child, the second worried parent. Play [[Lampwick's Mother|Carmela]] as emotionally direct (the players can use this) and [[Lampwick's Father|Emilio]] as emotionally blocked (they might need to work around this). The detail of the missing good boots is the tell: this was deliberate, not impulsive.

Don't resolve the Lampwick thread here — it pays off at [[Mangiafuoco's Theatre]] in a later chapter. Let it sit as an open wound.

The connection to Geppetto is warm and specific: Emilio has bought many puppets from him over the years, each one made for Lampwick. The two families have grown close the way neighbouring households do — slowly and without quite noticing. Lampwick grew up surrounded by Geppetto's work.
