# The Savings Bank

> [!info] Description
> A narrow two-storey building on the west side of the [[The Town Square|square]], with a brass plate beside the door and a door that sticks. Inside: two clerks working behind a long wooden counter in an atmosphere of enforced calm. The ledgers are perfect. The light is cool and even. Nothing that happens here is surprising — which is precisely why the clerks remember what wasn't.
>
> The deposit was expected. [[Geppetto]] is a regular, reliable, same time most weeks. The boy never came.

## Characters Here

- **Senior clerk** — thin, precise, faintly aggrieved, and waiting for someone to ask the right question. Genuinely useful if treated with patience; less so if rushed.
- **Second clerk** — consulted separately; was at her window at eight o'clock and saw [[Pinocchio]] on the *wrong side* of the square.

## What Players Can Learn

The puppet-boy never arrived. The absence has been noted in the log — a small, precise, damning entry.

The senior clerk was at his window at eight o'clock and saw the boy — not heading toward the bank, but on the **east side of the square**. Already heading away. Already the wrong direction.

**DC 11 Investigation:** The second clerk, separately consulted, adds: two strangers were walking alongside the boy — a fox with a cane and a cat who kept bumping into things. She watched them cross the square together heading east.

## GM Notes

The bank is a quick stop — two questions, two answers — but it provides the crucial geographical confirmation: [[Pinocchio]] was already on the east side of the square at eight o'clock, already going the wrong way. This locks the timeline and eliminates the possibility he went to the bank first and then got turned around. He was intercepted before he reached it.

Let the clerks be pleasantly bureaucratic. They are not dramatic. They are useful precisely because they are precise.
