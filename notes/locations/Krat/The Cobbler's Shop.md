# The Cobbler's Shop

> [!info] Description
> A ground-floor shopfront on the north side of the [[The Town Square|square]], with a worn step and a window full of lasts and leather. The cobbler is a broad, deliberate man who has worked this corner for twenty years. He is sitting behind his counter with a cup of tea going cold, waiting for the police to take a statement that the police have not yet come to take. He has filed a complaint about [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]]. Nothing has happened. He is quietly, continuously furious about this.
>
> The shop smells of leather and polish. Finished shoes line the wall behind the counter. A pair of lasts is mid-work on the bench, abandoned when things became more pressing.

## Characters Here

- [[The Cobbler]] — broad, deliberate, twenty years on this corner. The investigation's most efficient shortcut: he will name the Inn and point east before the players have finished their question.

## What Players Can Learn

The cobbler did **not** see [[Pinocchio]] — his view is partially blocked by the awning post. But he did not need to.

He knows [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] by sight. They have hit his shop before — a pair of shoes walked out while he was in the back. He filed a complaint. [[The Police Officer]] is aware. Nothing has been done.

He heard about the ruckus at the Red Crawfish Inn this morning. He connects it immediately. He says it plainly: *"If you're looking for the boy, you want the Inn. East side of the square. The crawfish sign above the door."*

He establishes the Fox and Cat as **known serial criminals with a pattern and a file** — not opportunists. This matters.

**[[Postwoman|Delia Conti]] connection:** A delivery arrived that morning. She mentioned the strangers. That's how the cobbler heard before the ruckus even resolved.

## GM Notes

The cobbler is the fastest route to the Inn in the entire investigation. A single conversation, handled directly, gives the players everything they need: the destination, the confirmation of the Fox and Cat's criminal history, and the emotional fact that the system has failed to protect anyone from them. His quiet fury is the most honest response to institutional failure in Chapter 1.

If the players have already been to two or three other locations and are starting to feel the investigation dragging, send them here.
