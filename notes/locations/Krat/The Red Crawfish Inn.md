# The Red Crawfish Inn

> [!info] Description
> The inn is on the east side of the [[The Town Square|square]], accessible through a side lane — a carved wooden crawfish above the door, paint faded but still red. Inside: low ceilings, amber lamplight, the smell of old wine and fried oil, and a fire that smokes badly. The room is long and narrow, with a bar along the left wall, tables in the main space, and a kitchen beyond a squeaky swinging door. Near the back, a corner table shows the remains of a large meal — three settings, two scraped clean, one barely touched. A wine glass on its side, not righted.
>
> The side door — the one [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] used — is visible from the right angle. It leads to the lane. It locks from the outside.

## Characters Here

- [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] — the innkeeper; a broad man in a stained apron, standing between the party and the door. He has been waiting. He wants twelve gold pieces.
- [[The Innkeeper's Wife]] — in the kitchen, through the squeaky door. The safety valve. The room's best witness.
- [[Rabbit]] — corner table; brown coat; piece of paper covered in very small, very neat handwriting. He has been here since this morning. He saw everything.

## What Players Can Learn

This is the chapter's destination — the place every thread leads.

**From [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] (post-fight, post-resolution):** The Fox and Cat arrived mid-morning with the boy. They ordered lavishly. Just before midday, the Fox announced — publicly, in a voice that carried — that everything was *"taken care of."* Then he and the Cat were gone through the side door. Twenty minutes later, Lucone came to collect. *"The boy looked confused, not guilty. He kept saying the Fox had settled it — said it carefully, like saying it more clearly would make it true. He wasn't lying. I've seen liars. He believed it. Then I raised my voice, and people looked, and he ran. East."*

**From [[The Innkeeper's Wife]]:** Watched through the serving hatch. The Fox's announcement. [[Pinocchio]] alone afterward, confused. The boy insisting carefully and sincerely. The side door [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] used. She is the most reliable witness and will say so, clearly and without embellishment, once the room is calm.

**From [[Rabbit]]:** Full documentation. The Fox's directions to the Field of Miracles (past the east gate, half a mile down the old road toward the hills). Description of both criminals. Accurate timeline. *"the boy is innocent — confirm on arrival."* He watched the Fox and Cat leave through the side door with no payment changing hands.

The direction is confirmed: east, past the east gate, toward the Field of Miracles.

## GM Notes

**The tutorial fight:** This is where combat happens. [[The Innkeeper|Mastro Lucone]] blocks the exit before asking nicely; the situation narrows. He has rolling-pin reach and the weight of thirty years hauling barrels. Use his full stat block. **Two kitchen staff as backup** if things go his way — [[The Chef|Taddeo Moretti]] (hot-tempered cook, dangerous near the kitchen doorway) and [[The Waitress|Rosa Ferrini]] (reluctant, can be persuaded to stand down mid-fight with DC 10 Persuasion). [[The Innkeeper's Wife|Vittoria]] intervenes if the party is losing badly — she comes through the squeaky door and stops it.

**The space is kinetic:** Tables flip for half cover (Action). Wine barrels are difficult terrain; can be rolled (DC 12 Athletics, forces a Dex save). The chandelier is just high enough to swing from (DC 13 Athletics; advantage on next attack). The cook appears through the kitchen door, takes in the scene, retreats; on a second look he has a ladle and is deciding whose side he's on.

**The bill:** Twelve gold pieces. Fight outcome shifts negotiation DC but doesn't cancel the bill.
- Win clearly: DC drops by 2.
- Wife intervenes: DC 14 Persuasion to reduce; **DC 11** if players acknowledge the genuine wrong done to Lucone.
- Party lost: DC 16; possible; collateral accepted.
- Pay in full: Lucone accepts, mollified, tells everything.
- Work it off: Three hours. During it, **DC 12 Perception** spots [[Rabbit]] in the corner — very still, piece of paper, very small neat handwriting.

**DC 14 Insight on Lucone:** He is genuinely wronged *and* not above leveraging the situation. He could have stopped the boy at the door. He didn't.
