# The Academy

> [!info] Description
> A tall building one block north of the [[The Town Square|square]], with high windows that let in thin grey light and a door that opens on a smell of chalk and quiet attention. The desks inside are in perfect rows. A spelling-chart on the wall. The register on Serafina's desk is immaculate — every attendance, every absence, every unexplained gap noted in precise ink. The room feels like a place where facts are taken seriously, and where someone has cared about each name in that register for a very long time.
>
> Today two desks are empty.

## Characters Here

- [[The teacher|Serafina Conti]] — warm and watchful, with a register that is never wrong. She took attendance. She noted the absences. She sent word to [[Geppetto]]. She has specific, careful things to say about [[Lampwick]] — and the weight of someone who wishes she had said them sooner.

## What Players Can Learn

[[Pinocchio]] was not at school. Recorded in the register: *ABSENT — no message sent.* An unexplained absence is a serious matter.

**[[Lampwick]] is also absent.** And this worries Serafina differently — specifically. [[Lampwick]] has been distracted lately. *Obsessed* with the idea of running away to become a puppeteer. The Mangiafuoco posters have lit something in him; he's been talking about nothing else for weeks. His absences lately have felt less like truancy and more like *preparation*. Serafina does not think [[Lampwick]] was taken. She thinks [[Lampwick]] left.

The register entry for [[Lampwick]]: *ABSENT — third time this month. No message. Increasing concern.*

## GM Notes

The Academy is optional for reaching the Inn — the players don't need it — but it is **essential for the campaign's larger shape**. Serafina's specific word (*"obsessed"*) and her observation that the absences felt like preparation rather than truancy are the key seeds. They will germinate when the players reach [[Mangiafuoco's Theatre]] in a later chapter and find [[Lampwick]] inside it, technically there by choice and practically unable to leave.

Don't let anyone pull the Lampwick thread to resolution here. Let it sit. Let the players remember it later.

The Mangiafuoco poster connection — Serafina can confirm the posters were the fixation — links [[The Town Square]] and the school into the same atmosphere of foreshadowing.
