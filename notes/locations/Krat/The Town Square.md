# The Town Square

> [!info] Description
> The square opens up between a cloth merchant's awning and a fish-seller's stall, loud with late-morning trade. Pigeons scatter between the wheels of a slow cart. The cobblestones are close-fitted and smooth; a dropped coin would ring here and everyone within twenty feet would hear it. Around the square's edges, nailed to posts and shuttered shopfronts: colourful printed posters advertising *The Puppet Theatre of Mangiafuoco* — bright reds and golds, a grinning fire-eater's face, dates suggesting the show is coming soon. Professionally printed. Eye-catching. Several have already been torn down by children who wanted to keep them.
>
> The square sits at the centre of Krat's daily life. The bank is to the west. The cobbler faces from the north. The school is one block north. The inn is east. South leads back to the residential streets.

## Characters Here

- [[The Flower Seller|Fiamma]] — the flower seller, south entry of the square. Forthcoming, opinionated, and a reliable witness who considers gossip a public service.
- [[Newspaper Seller|Nico]] — the newspaper seller, northwest corner. Sharp, wry, slightly wary. Noticed the Fox said [[Pinocchio]]'s name before the boy gave it.
- [[Postwoman|Delia Conti]] — the postwoman, mid-round; passing through. Helpful but pressed — she has a route to finish. Remembers the Fox's limp that stopped at the corner.
- [[The Police Officer]] — encountered here or nearby. Unhelpful, contemptuous, and probably knows more than he's sharing.

## What Players Can Learn

Three witnesses saw what happened. Separately, each account is partial. Together they build a complete picture: [[The Fox]] and [[The Cat]] approached [[Pinocchio]] near the centre of the square, *knew his name before he gave it*, and walked with him east toward the Inn.

- **[[The Flower Seller|Fiamma]]:** The coin fell. Two strangers appeared. *"The fox was very charming — the sort that makes your purse feel lighter just from standing nearby."* She watched all three walk east.
- **[[Newspaper Seller|Nico]]:** Heard the Fox say [[Pinocchio]]'s name before [[Pinocchio]] said it himself. Recognised a mark being worked. Watched them go into the Inn together.
- **[[Postwoman|Delia Conti]]:** *"Silver-topped cane, well-dressed, feigning a limp. Couldn't tell you what happened to the limp when they reached the corner."*

The players don't need all three accounts — any two give the essential information. The third adds texture. [[The Police Officer]] will grudgingly confirm the Fox and Cat are a known problem; he wraps every answer in faint contempt.

**The Mangiafuoco Posters:** Environmental foreshadowing. One features [[Pinocchio]]'s name in a testimonial that is almost certainly faked. A detail now; significant later.

## GM Notes

The square is the investigation's hub — the place the party returns to as they fan out to the bank, cobbler, and school. Let the witnesses be distinct: [[The Flower Seller|Fiamma]] is forthcoming and opinionated; [[Newspaper Seller|Nico]] is wry and slightly wary; [[Postwoman|Delia]] is helpful but pressed — she has a route to finish. The key fact to land is that the Fox knew [[Pinocchio]]'s name before being told it. That implies research or a tip-off.

The Mangiafuoco posters are background detail in Chapter 1 and a neon sign in retrospect.
