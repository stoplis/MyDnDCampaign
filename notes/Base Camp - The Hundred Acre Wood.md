> [!abstract] At a Glance
> The Hundred Acre Wood is the party's home base between chapters. It is safe, warm, and entirely unlike Pinocchio's world. Every time a Pooh character is found and brought home, the party levels up. Right now it is quiet and empty. That is temporary.

---

# What It Feels Like

The Wood arrives differently from any place in Pinocchio's world. There is no gate, no threshold — the trees simply change, becoming older and more comfortable-looking, the light turning amber even at midday, the air carrying the smell of honey and wood smoke and autumn. The ground is softer here. The stream is unhurried. Even the shadows are gentle.

When the Wood is full, it is the most alive place in the world. When it is empty, it is the saddest place in the world, which is a different kind of alive.

> [!tip] GM Tone Note
> When the party arrives at the Wood — especially the first time — let it breathe. Slow the description down. Let the smell and the light land. After the road, after Pinocchio's world, this should feel like relief. That relief is what makes the emptiness hurt, and the hurt is what makes the returns matter.

# Mechanics
## Milestone Levelling

The Wood is where level-ups happen. Not in the field. Not mid-dungeon. Here.

Each time a Pooh character is found and brought home, the Wood comes back a little more, and the party levels up. This is milestone levelling — no XP tracking, no arbitrary numbers. The party grows stronger because they have done something real and the Wood knows it.

Frame this narratively. The level-up is not a mechanical event that happens to interrupt the story; the level-up *is* the story. A light, a smell, a sound in the trees. The Wood recognises what the party has done. The party feels more capable, more ready. Then you tell them to update their sheets.

## The Spider Merchant

![[Spider Mechant.PNG]]

*A large spider, impeccably turned out, wearing a small hat with a flower tucked into the band. She has set up a tidy stall at the base of a particular oak — a folding table, a velvet-lined case, and a handwritten sign that reads* CURIOUS WARES — REASONABLE PRICES. *She is extremely friendly. She will not tell you her name. She seems to have been expecting you.*

The Spider Merchant appears in the Wood between chapters and is always ready to trade. She does not explain where the items come from. She does not need to.

> [!info] Mechanic — Chapter Tokens
> At the end of each chapter, each party member receives **1 Chapter Token** as a reward for what they've accomplished. Tokens can be spent at the Spider Merchant's stall before the next chapter begins. They do not carry over indefinitely — unspent tokens expire when the next chapter's token is issued.
>
> Each player may spend their token on **one item** from the stall. Tokens are personal — they cannot be pooled or transferred.

> [!note] The Spider's Patter
> She has a line for everything. If asked about provenance: *"Fell off a wagon."* If asked if an item is safe: *"Mostly."* If asked if she is really a spider: *"What a curious question. That will be two tokens."* She seems to find all of this delightful.

## Party Upgrades — The Wood Gives Back

Each time a Pooh character comes home, the Wood heals a little — and so does the party. These upgrades are permanent, apply to every party member, and stack. They are not magic items. They are gifts, habits, and practical knowledge passed from someone who is genuinely, unreservedly glad to be home.

Frame each upgrade narratively before announcing the mechanical effect. The character giving the gift should not know it is a mechanical upgrade. They are simply doing what they would do.

> [!tip] GM Note
> Announce the flavour first. Let it land. Then say: *"From now on, whenever you..."* The players should feel the gift before they feel the mechanic.

### The Upgrade Table

| Rescued | Upgrade | Story | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[Roo]] | **Kanga's Medical Kit** | Kanga says very little when she sees Roo. She simply gets on with things. She presses a small wrapped parcel into each of their hands and says *"for later."* She does not make a fuss. | Each party member begins every chapter with **one free Potion of Healing**. Kanga restocks them quietly before departure. Unused ones from the previous chapter vanish — she takes them back and refreshes them. |
| [[Piglet]] | **Piglet's Reserves** | Piglet is not sure he was very helpful while he was captured. He says this several times. # The Locations

## Pooh's House — "Mr Sanders"

![[Pooh’s house.PNG]]

*A single-room dwelling at the base of a beech tree, accessed through a low round door. A sign above the door reads* MR SANDERS. *Inside: one chair, one table, one fireplace, a great many honey jars.*

When empty: door ajar, honey pots knocked over (one shattered), a smackerel half-eaten, a note on the table: *"Gone out. Back soon."* The note was meant to be true.

When occupied: warm and slightly sticky underfoot near the kitchen. A Passive Perception 14 notices the hidden shelf behind the fireplace brick — Pooh's most special honey jar, kept for emergencies, which is to say for Tuesdays.

## Piglet's House — Trespassers W

![[Piglets house.PNG]]

*A small old beech tree, sign reading* TRESPASSERS W *(the rest having blown away). Pressed flowers in frames. A kettle. A very small armchair.*

When empty: a brave little note on the door: *"Gone to find help. Back soon. — Piglet."* The armchair is empty. The flowers are undisturbed.

When occupied: the door is only 2.5 feet high — Medium or larger creatures must squeeze to enter. Piglet's emergency hazel-nuts are under the floorboards (DC 13 Perception). He uses them in dire situations, which by his definition includes most Wednesdays.

## Rabbit's Warren and Garden

![[Rabbits house - front.PNG]]

*An extensive burrow with a front door, back door, a side door for guests who overstay, and several windows Rabbit installed himself. The garden is immaculate: carrots in precise rows, a compost situation Rabbit takes personally.*

When empty: organised, tidy, a half-eaten supper on the kitchen table. A second pencil on the desk beside an unfinished list. The list should not be read; it is private.

When occupied: Rabbit's larder contains a truly extraordinary quantity of vegetables and a tidy inventory ledger. Players who read it learn that Pooh has been taking honey "on loan" since March and the debt has been accumulating interest.

## The Thoughtful Spot

*A log at the edge of the Wood beside a slow stream, worn smooth from use. The grass is soft. The sound of wind in high trees. It is not much to look at.*

This is where the party should begin and end each return to the Wood. It is the Wood's centre of gravity — the place things start and the place things return to.

A Short Rest taken here restores one additional Hit Die. Some places are good for the spirit. This is one of them.

## Kanga and Roo's House

![[Kangas house.PNG]]

*A neat, well-maintained house in the southeast of the Wood, near the Sandy Pit. It smells of something clean and warm. There is always a spare plate at the table.*

When empty (both gone): Roo's muddy paw-prints still track from the Sandy Pit. The spare plate is set. Under the sand of the Sandy Pit (DC 14 Investigation) is a forgotten toy of Roo's — a small carved donkey. In this campaign, it carries a strange weight. Kanga's medical cabinet (DC 10 with her permission, DC 17 without) contains supplies equivalent to a Healer's Kit and one Potion of Healing.

When Roo is home: paw-prints reappear fresh. The Sandy Pit is recently disturbed.

When Kanga is home: the house is warm, the plate is freshly set, and there is a smell of something being carefully prepared. Kanga does not make a fuss about being home. She simply gets on with things.

## The Field of Miracles

![[The Field of Miracles.PNG]]

*A clearing at the eastern edge of the Wood, beautiful and wrong. The light is too golden. The soil is too dark. The silence is too complete. Nothing grows in the patches of earth at its centre.*

The Field is not the Wood's. It predates it, or exists alongside it — a place that was already there when the Wood grew up around it. The Fox and Cat knew of it. This suggests something the party may eventually want to ask: who told them?

The clearing functions as the chapter 2 encounter space and thereafter as an occasional ominous location. It does not get better when the Wood heals. It simply gets quieter.

## Owl's House — The Chestnuts

![[Owls house.PNG]]

*A large, grand tree residence with a proper letterbox (which Owl cannot reliably read, but tries), a doorknocker, and a bell-pull. The interior is filled with framed family portraits, dried grasses, and long explanatory notes Owl has left himself.*

When empty: the framed portrait of "my uncle Robert" is actually a picture of a different owl that Owl has simply decided is his uncle. DC 15 Investigation in the library uncovers genuine Wood lore.

When occupied: Owl is mid-sentence before the door fully opens. He will tell the party something genuinely useful, embedded in something very long. DC 12 Insight to identify the useful part.

## Eeyore's Gloomy Place — Boggy and Sad

![[Eeyores house.PNG]]

*A damp, reedy corner near a boggy stream. Eeyore's twig house stands here. The ground squelches. Thistles grow, which Eeyore considers a point in its favour.*

Difficult terrain throughout. Any character who sits quietly here for ten minutes with nothing to do gains a particular kind of peace — not happiness, but company in melancholy. No mechanical effect. This is a roleplay note.

When occupied: Eeyore is probably here, and probably expecting something to go wrong, and probably correct. He is, in a strange way, one of the most comfortable presences in the Wood.

## Tigger’s Home

![[Tiggers house.PNG]]

*A tall, narrow house built up the side of a tree in a way that makes structural engineers uneasy. The door is painted orange and black in bold stripes. There is a spring-loaded letterbox that has, on at least one occasion, launched correspondence across the Wood. A sign reads* T-I-G-G-E-R *in letters of varying size, which is very like him.*

When empty: the door bounces open when you knock — it was never quite latched. Inside, the furniture has been rearranged since anyone was last here. Chairs are upended. A rug is concertinaed against one wall. The ceiling has scuff marks that can only have been made from below. On the kitchen table, a half-full jar of Extract of Malt with a spoon still in it — Tigger’s only food, and enthusiastically preferred. The house has the feeling of a place that was recently, vigorously occupied and then suddenly wasn’t.

When occupied: you hear him before you see him. A thudding from somewhere above, a crash, a brief silence, and then the door opens from the inside with some force. Tigger will ask what you think you are doing today. When told, he will immediately have strong opinions. He is extremely loud. He will bounce during most of the conversation. He means all of it warmly.

> [!tip] Tigger’s Particular Usefulness
> Tigger knows the Wood better than anyone at speed — he has been through every part of it at least once, horizontally, while not quite in control of his trajectory. If the party needs to move quickly through the Wood or the surrounding area, Tigger can guide them. He will describe the route in terms of things he bounced off. This is, surprisingly, accurate.

# Long Rests in the Wood

The Wood is completely safe. No hostile encounter can begin here. The party can take a Long Rest freely and at any time. Hostile NPCs who pursue the party to the Wood's edge simply... stop. They cannot account for why, afterward.

> [!info] Safe Haven Rule
> No combat encounter can be initiated within the Wood's boundary. This is not a rule the party is told; it is simply what happens. The Wood is what it is.

---

# Running the Wood Between Chapters

The Wood should take 20–30 minutes of table time between chapters — not a full session, but a real breath. Let the party:

- Move through the empty locations and observe the changes from the previous return
- Take their Long Rest and level up
- Talk to whatever Pooh characters are currently home
- Ask whatever questions the chapter left open

The Wood is not a puzzle. It does not need to be solved. It needs to be *inhabited* for a little while before the party goes back into Pinocchio's world.

> [!tip] The Contrast is the Point
> The more vivid the Wood, the harder Pinocchio's world lands. The more Pinocchio's world costs, the more the Wood matters. These two settings define each other. Let them.
