# What Actually Happened

> [!note] What Actually Happened — Tigger
>
> **Before.**
>
> The wonderful thing about Tiggers is that Tiggers are wonderful things. Their tops are made out of rubber. Their bottoms are made out of springs. They're bouncy, trouncy, flouncy, pouncy, fun, fun, fun, fun, fun. The most wonderful thing about Tiggers is that I'm the only one.
>
> I knew this. This was the thing I knew.
>
> ---
>
> **The first day.**
>
> They put me in a very small wagon. The wagon had bars. Bars are not bouncy things. There is no room in a wagon with bars to do a proper bounce — not a full one, not the kind that takes you up and over and around and back. I tried to explain this to the man with the hat. He said: *"Wonderful. We'll call it the Pacing Tiger."* I said: *"I'm not a tiger, I'm a TIGGER."* He said: *"Yes, yes."* He wrote something in a ledger. The woman with the ledger was the one writing in the ledger. They both wrote in the ledger. They wrote for quite a long time.
>
> ---
>
> **Later.**
>
> The shows started. I was supposed to stay in the ring and be looked at. This was the act: being looked at. I pointed out that this was not very Tigger-ish — the whole point of a Tigger is the bouncing, everyone knows that. They said the bouncing was a problem. They said the bouncing was *unpredictable*. They said *unpredictable* the way you say something you have decided is wrong.
>
> ---
>
> **Much later.**
>
> I cannot remember whether it was a Tuesday. I used to know what day it was. Christopher Robin always knew, and I asked him, and he told me, and then I forgot and asked again because that is also a Tigger thing. But Christopher Robin is not here. And there are no days in a small wagon, only shows.
>
> I bounce a little, in the corner, sometimes. Very small. Just to remember.
>
> ---
>
> **The thing they did.**
>
> I don't know when they did it exactly. It was not one thing. It was many small things over many shows — the lights and the music and the way they said *"stay"* and *"wait"* and *"not yet"* and *"that's enough"* until those words were the shape of everything and bouncing was a thing that happened elsewhere, to someone else, in a Wood I was not sure was real.
>
> They gave me the gloves. They said the gloves were for a new act. They said I was wonderful. They said it the way you say it when you don't mean it, but I could not remember how I knew the difference.
>
> ---
>
> **After.**
>
> The wonderful thing about Tiggers—
>
> The wonderful thing—
>
> I am still the only one.

# Chapter Design

> [!abstract] GM Overview
> The Wandering Circus of Marvels has arrived near Krat. From outside, it is spectacular: bright and loud and aimed at children. The wagon wheel logo is on every poster. The players have seen that symbol before.
>
> Fenwick and Celestine — the Ringmasters — are running a sorting operation. Children who perform well enough go to Pleasure Island. The Circus is the audition room.
>
> Tigger is here. He has been here for some time.
>
> The chapter's structural pivot is the arena. Once the players step into the ring, the tent seals and the show begins. This is not a dungeon. This is theatre — theatre that wants to hurt them, theatre that grows increasingly angry when it fails to. The Ringmasters are the audience and the antagonists and the final act, in that order.

# Approaching the Circus

> [!info] Scene: The Grounds
> The Wandering Circus of Marvels has raised its big top in the field east of the road, visible from half a mile away. It is enormous — striped red and gold, pennants on every peak, ropes and rigging giving it the silhouette of something that arrived by sea. The smell of animals, sawdust, and fried food reaches the party well before the gates.
>
> The circus grounds are full of the movement of an operation in progress: roustabouts carrying equipment, animals being led between enclosures, performers rehearsing at the edges of the field. Everything is professional. Nothing is quite right.
>
> **Things the party notices if they look:**
> - The children performing in rehearsal have the focused, mechanical quality of people who have been drilled rather than trained. The joy is gone but the skill remains.
> - The wagon wheel logo is everywhere — on banners, on the sides of wagons, on the performers' costumes as a small embroidered detail.
> - An enclosure near the back, larger than the others, with bars on the wagon. Something orange moves inside.
> - In the VIP tent near the main entrance: two distinctive figures with drinks, watching the rehearsals with the satisfaction of people whose day is going exactly as planned.

> [!example] The Fox and Cat — In the Stands
> The Fox and Cat are here as employees of the Circus — talent scouts and intermediaries, not performers. They have good seats. They will see the party immediately.
>
> The Fox will come to the rail with a warm smile. *"What a surprise. Again. You do seem to turn up."* He will not tell them what the Circus does with the children. He will tell them the show is tonight. He will suggest they stay for it.
>
> The Cat watches.
>
> **If the party pursues them after the arena fight:** The Fox and Cat slip through a backstage exit when the ringmasters fall. Whether the party can catch them before they disappear depends on the players. If caught: the Fox has a manifest — he will trade it for safe passage. It lists the names of children "transferred" to Pleasure Island.

# Into the Big Top

> [!info] Scene: Before the Show
> The big top interior is designed to overwhelm: tiered seating, a sawdust ring sixty feet across, rigging above, the smell of canvas and animal and something that might be excitement or anxiety. The ring is lit from above by a ring of lanterns. The seats are empty. The show has not started.
>
> Players who explore before the show may find:
> - **The backstage corridor** (DC 12 Perception or Investigation): a long passage behind the seating, lined with wagons. One wagon is locked with three padlocks. Something inside it moves, very slightly.
> - **The animal handlers** (DC 10 Insight): working normally, a little too quickly, not making eye contact with the party.
> - **The Ringmasters' box** (visible from the ring floor): elevated, private, draped in red and gold. A small table with a ledger. Two chairs. Both empty.

> [!warning] The Trap — Entering the Ring
> At some point — whether the party enters the ring directly, whether they are ushered in as "special guests," whether they step in while investigating — **the moment all party members have crossed the sawdust ring boundary, the tent seals.**
>
> Not loudly. A low thrum, like a bass note too low to hear. The exits are still visible. They simply cannot be reached. A DC 15 Arcana check identifies the effect: a binding circle, worked into the sawdust itself, activated by presence. Dispelling it would require more power than the party currently has access to. The ring is the stage, and the stage has a performer now.
>
> Above: the lanterns brighten. The rigging shifts. Two spotlights converge on the Ringmasters' box.

# The Grand Spectacle

## The Ringmasters' Entrance

> [!example] Fenwick and Celestine — The Introduction
> Fenwick descends to the box with the speed and timing of a man who has made every entrance count for forty years. His top hat is immaculate. His coat is red and gold. His voice fills the tent.
>
> *"Ladies and gentlemen—"*
>
> Celestine, taking her seat and opening her ledger: *"—there are no ladies and gentlemen present—"*
>
> *"—our distinguished guests—"* (a slight adjustment) *"—have chosen to join us this evening for a most exclusive performance."*
>
> *"One night only."*
>
> *"The finest acts in the—"*
>
> *"—in the world—"*
>
> *"—have been assembled for your entertainment. We do so hope—"*
>
> *"—you'll find it—"*
>
> *"—illuminating."*
>
> A long pause. Fenwick's smile is magnificent and does not reach his eyes.
>
> *"Do try to last."*

> [!tip] GM Note — The Commentary Track
> Throughout the arena fight, Fenwick and Celestine are the colour commentators. Every wave has an introduction. Every wave defeat earns a response. Track the tone carefully — it starts as theatre and ends as something unguarded and personal.
>
> Fenwick does most of the talking. Celestine provides the punctuation. When things go badly, their rhythms fall out of sync. When they are furious, they stop finishing each other's sentences entirely.

## The Wheel of Wonders

> [!info] Mechanic — The Wheel of Wonders
> Between each wave, Fenwick announces the Wheel of Wonders with the enthusiasm of a man who has not yet decided whether he finds this entertaining or insulting. A large spinning wheel descends from the rigging — ornate, gilded, eight segments. One party member spins it.
>
> *"The Wheel is, of course, completely random. We are a circus, not a bakery."*
>
> Celestine, writing something in her ledger: *"Completely random."*
>
> Roll 1d8 or let the player spin physically if you have a wheel prop:

| Roll | Segment | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Healing Humour** | Each party member regains 2d6 HP. |
| 2 | **The Restoring Spin** | Each party member may spend up to 2 hit dice immediately (no short rest required). |
| 3 | **The Mender's Gift** | The party member with the lowest current HP is restored to half their maximum. |
| 4 | **The Caster's Favour** | Each spellcaster recovers their lowest expended spell slot. |
| 5 | **The Lion's Courage** | The party has advantage on saving throws until the end of the next wave. |
| 6 | **The Crowd's Delight** | One party member of the DM's choice gains advantage on all attack rolls for the first round of the next wave. |
| 7 | **The Showstopper** | One party member (player's choice) recovers all uses of one class feature that recharges on a short rest. |
| 8 | **The Prat Fall** | *"Oh, that's unfortunate."* One party member (nearest the ring's centre) takes 2d4 bludgeoning damage and falls prone. The Ringmasters find this very funny. The rest of the result is otherwise positive — the party gains a free short rest immediately after the laughter dies. |

> [!tip] GM Note — Using the Wheel
> The Prat Fall (result 8) should ideally happen once and only once, for comedic maximum. If it comes up a second time, Fenwick sighs as if even the wheel's cruelty is now predictable. Celestine writes something down.

## Wave 1 — The Clowns

> [!warning] Wave 1: The Clowns
> *"The first act,"* Fenwick announces, *"is a perennial favourite. Simple. Warm. Accessible to all ages."*
>
> *"We start with clowns,"* says Celestine, without looking up.
>
> Three figures emerge from the smoke — not walking through an entrance but coalescing from the sawdust, assembling themselves upward. They are dressed as clowns. They fight like something that learned to fight by watching clowns fight and decided that was exactly the right approach.

> [!info] Stat Block — Circus Clown (×3)
> **CR** 1/2 | **Type** Humanoid | **Size** Medium
>
> | STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
> |-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
> | 10 (+0) | 15 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 9 (-1) | 8 (-1) | 13 (+1) |
>
> **HP:** 16 | **AC:** 12 (painted leather, inexplicably)
>
> **Traits:**
> - *Unnerving.* Creatures attempting to predict a Clown's movement or attack have disadvantage on the relevant checks or saves. They are simply not consistent.
> - *Pratfall.* When the Clown takes damage, it may use its reaction to fall dramatically prone and make one creature within 5 ft. succeed on a DC 11 Dexterity saving throw or also fall prone.
>
> **Actions:**
> - *Juggling Strike.* +4 to hit, range 20/40 ft., one target. Hit: 1d6+2 bludgeoning (thrown wooden club).
> - *Seltzer Bottle (Recharge 5–6).* The Clown sprays a creature within 10 ft. The target must succeed on a DC 11 Constitution saving throw or be blinded until the end of its next turn.
>
> **When defeated:** The clown does not die. It deflates — slowly, with a long descending noise — and melts back into smoke and sawdust. There is no corpse.

> [!note] Ringmaster Reaction — After Wave 1
> *"Excellent! Excellent form. You are, we note—"*
>
> *"—performing well,"* Celestine finishes. Toneless.
>
> *"Which is, of course, what we like to see. Fenwick, the wheel."*
>
> **Spin the Wheel of Wonders.**

## Wave 2 — The Woozles

> [!warning] Wave 2: Huffalumps and Woozles
> *"The second act,"* Fenwick announces, with the careful pacing of someone performing calm, *"features creatures from the deepest wilderness. Rarely seen. Never tamed."*
>
> *"Rarely real,"* says Celestine, making a note.
>
> Four shapes boil up from the smoke — larger now, hunched, wrong-shaped in the way of things seen at the edge of sleep. They look something like the weasels the party has faced before, but larger, stranger, their features blurred as if the memory of them is imprecise. Their eyes are yellow. They make a sound like something thinking hard about being frightening.
>
> The Ringmasters called them Huffalumps and Woozles. They are not. But they are made from the same material fear is made from, and they are real enough to hurt.

> [!info] Stat Block — Woozle (×4)
> **CR** 1 | **Type** Illusion (Beast statistics) | **Size** Medium
>
> | STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
> |-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
> | 14 (+2) | 14 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 5 (-3) | 8 (-1) | 6 (-2) |
>
> **HP:** 18 | **AC:** 13 (natural)
>
> **Traits:**
> - *Smoke-Born.* When a Woozle is reduced to 0 HP, it does not fall — it unravels upward into smoke. No corpse. A DC 12 Arcana check confirms it was not truly alive; a DC 12 Nature check confirms it was not truly a beast either. It was something made of the idea of a beast.
> - *Pack Hunter.* The Woozle has advantage on attack rolls against a creature if at least one other Woozle is within 5 ft. of the target.
>
> **Actions:**
> - *Claw.* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 1d6+2 slashing.
>
> **Scaling:** For a harder encounter, add a fifth Woozle or give one of them 30 HP as a Huffalump variant.

> [!note] Ringmaster Reaction — After Wave 2
> There is a pause before Fenwick speaks. A beat too long.
>
> *"Remarkable. Most—"*
>
> *"—most,"* Celestine agrees, and there is something clipped in it.
>
> *"Most guests find the second act more... conclusive. You are to be commended."* A thin smile. *"Do enjoy the wheel."*
>
> **Spin the Wheel of Wonders.**

## Wave 3 — The Acrobats

> [!warning] Wave 3: The Acrobat Enforcers
> *"The third act,"* Fenwick says, and his voice is precisely the same as it was for acts one and two. *"Requires a great deal of training."*
>
> Celestine has stopped writing. She is watching the ring.
>
> Three figures drop from the rigging — not from smoke this time, but from above, landing on the sawdust in a formation. They wear the Circus colours. They have been doing this for years. They work together the way people who have been hurt together work: efficiently, without communication, each movement implying the next.

> [!info] Stat Block — Acrobat Enforcer (×3)
> **CR** 1 | **Type** Humanoid | **Size** Medium
>
> | STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
> |-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
> | 13 (+1) | 17 (+3) | 13 (+1) | 10 (+0) | 12 (+1) | 11 (+0) |
>
> **HP:** 22 | **AC:** 14 (acrobat's reflexes)
> **Skills:** Acrobatics +7, Athletics +3, Perception +3
>
> **Traits:**
> - *Aerial Strike.* If the Acrobat moves at least 15 ft. before attacking (including from a jump or rigging swing), the attack deals an additional 1d6 damage.
> - *Tumbling Dodge.* As a reaction when an attacker the Acrobat can see hits it, it halves the attack's damage.
>
> **Actions:**
> - *Twin Batons (Multiattack).* The Acrobat makes two attacks.
> - *Baton.* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 1d6+3 bludgeoning.
>
> **Terrain:** The rigging above is reachable with a DC 13 Athletics check and can be used against the acrobats — if a character can get above them, they gain advantage on attacks and the Aerial Strike bonus themselves.
>
> **When defeated:** The acrobats are humans. They fall. They do not melt. After the wave ends, if any are unconscious rather than dead, they can be stabilised and may be questioned. They know only the next two days' schedule; they don't know where the children go after the Circus.

> [!note] Ringmaster Reaction — After Wave 3
> The pause is longer this time. Fenwick taps his cane against the rail once.
>
> *"Fenwick,"* Celestine says.
>
> *"I see it."*
>
> *"The wheel,"* she says, flatly.
>
> *"Yes."* He reaches for the rigging pull. He does not smile. *"The wheel."*
>
> **Spin the Wheel of Wonders.**

## Wave 4 — Tigger

> [!warning] Wave 4: The Tigger
> There is no announcement.
>
> Instead, Fenwick straightens, adjusts his hat, and says, in a voice that is almost completely level: *"We have been saving our most... reliable act."*
>
> *"Our most special attraction,"* Celestine adds, and she finally looks up from her ledger.
>
> The large wagon at the edge of the ring rolls forward on its own — pushed from behind by two silent roustabouts who do not meet the party's eyes. It stops at the ring's boundary. The padlocks fall.
>
> The door opens.
>
> He is wearing boxing gloves. They are enormous — comically large, bright red, the kind of gloves you would choose if you had decided gloves should be as much like paws as possible. He walks into the ring slowly, which is not how Tigger walks. He looks at the party without recognition, which is not how Tigger looks at anyone. His eyes are slightly unfocused, as though he is listening to something the party cannot hear.
>
> He is not bouncing.

> [!info] Stat Block — Tigger (Hypnotised)
> **CR** 2 | **Type** Humanoid | **Size** Medium
>
> | STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
> |-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
> | 16 (+3) | 14 (+2) | 14 (+2) | 8 (-1) | 6 (-2) | 14 (+2) |
>
> **HP:** 32 | **AC:** 12
>
> **Traits:**
> - *Hypnotised.* Tigger is under a conditioned trance. He does not respond to verbal commands from strangers. He fights because he has been told to fight. The commands are in the music — a low, constant tone from the rigging above. A DC 14 Perception check identifies it.
> - *The Trance Can Be Broken.* A player who spends their action speaking directly to Tigger — calling him by name, referencing something specific to him (the Wood, Christopher Robin, bouncing, Roo, his identity) — may attempt a **DC 12 Charisma (Persuasion)** check. On a success, Tigger stops mid-motion, looks at his gloves, looks at the ring, and the trance begins to lift. No further checks needed if the party holds still.
> - *Tigger Bounce.* Tigger may move up to 20 ft. as part of a charge. If he does, his attack has advantage.
>
> **Actions:**
> - *Boxing Combo (Multiattack).* Tigger makes two Boxing Glove attacks.
> - *Boxing Glove.* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 1d6+3 bludgeoning.
> - *Pounce (Recharge 5–6).* Tigger leaps up to 20 ft. onto a creature. The target must succeed on a **DC 13 Strength saving throw** or be knocked prone and restrained (escape DC 13) until the end of its next turn.
>
> **If the trance breaks:** Tigger stops. He looks at his boxing gloves for a long moment. Then he says, very quietly: *"...these aren't Tigger things."* He pulls them off. He flexes his hands. He looks around the ring. He looks up at the rigging. His jaw sets.
>
> *"They made me do that,"* he says. Not a question.
>
> Tigger is now an ally. He cannot fight the ringmasters effectively on his own — his conditioning is still lifting — but he **joins the party for the final wave** and fights alongside them. His bounce is coming back.

> [!note] Ringmaster Reaction — After Wave 4 (Trance Broken)
> If the trance breaks: a silence. Then a crack — Fenwick's cane hitting the railing. Hard.
>
> *"That—"*
>
> Celestine, quietly: *"—was not in the programme."*
>
> *"No."* His voice is completely flat. *"It was not."*
>
> *"Fenwick."*
>
> *"I know."*
>
> A beat. They look at each other — one of those looks that contain thirty years of partnership without needing words. Celestine closes her ledger. Fenwick removes his hat and sets it on the railing.
>
> *"I suppose,"* he says, *"we should finish this ourselves."*
>
> **No wheel. They walk into the ring.**

> [!note] Ringmaster Reaction — After Wave 4 (Tigger Defeated)
> If Tigger is defeated rather than freed: Fenwick brightens. Almost.
>
> *"There we are. Order. Performance. Outcome."*
>
> *"Wheel,"* says Celestine. She is writing again.
>
> **Spin the Wheel of Wonders. Then the ringmasters enter the ring.**

## Final Wave — The Ringmasters

> [!warning] Final Wave: Fenwick and Celestine
> There is no introduction. There is no announcement. Fenwick simply descends the stairs from the box to the ring floor, and Celestine walks at his side, ledger tucked under one arm, and they step into the sawdust together.
>
> The performance is over. What remains is the people.
>
> Fenwick rolls up his sleeves. Celestine opens the ledger one last time, reviews a page, and closes it with finality.
>
> *"We have,"* says Fenwick, *"been doing this for a long time."*
>
> *"Longer than you have been alive,"* Celestine adds.
>
> *"We have survived difficult crowds, bad seasons, a fire in Millhaven, and three separate attempts by the Coachman's competitors to—"*
>
> *"—to buy us out,"* she supplies.
>
> *"Buy us out. We have not survived this long by being—"*
>
> *"—sentimental."*
>
> *"No."*
>
> They take their positions at opposite sides of the ring.

> [!info] Stat Block — Fenwick, The Showman
> **CR** 3 | **Type** Humanoid | **Role** Striker / Controller
>
> | STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
> |-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
> | 13 (+1) | 14 (+2) | 14 (+2) | 14 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 18 (+4) |
>
> **HP:** 52 | **AC:** 13 (theatrical coat, surprisingly reinforced)
> **Skills:** Deception +6, Intimidation +6, Performance +8, Persuasion +8
>
> **Traits:**
> - *Centre of Attention.* While Fenwick is conscious, attack rolls against Celestine have disadvantage from any creature that can see Fenwick. He ensures he is always visible.
> - *The Show Must Go On.* When Fenwick is reduced to half HP or below, he gains 1d8 extra damage on all attacks until the end of the encounter. His performance gets bigger as conditions worsen.
> - *Professional Pride.* If a player character comments that his performance is *bad, mediocre, underwhelming, or could be done better* — including as a taunt — Fenwick must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or use his reaction to make one immediate melee attack against the speaker, regardless of whose turn it is.
>
> **Actions:**
> - *Multiattack.* Fenwick makes two Ringmaster's Cane attacks.
> - *Ringmaster's Cane.* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 1d8+2 bludgeoning.
> - *Voice of the Big Top (Recharge 5–6).* Fenwick's voice swells to arena-filling volume. Every creature within 30 ft. must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or be stunned until the end of its next turn. This shakes the rigging.
>
> **Bonus Action:**
> - *Commanding Entrance.* Fenwick moves up to 15 ft. without provoking opportunity attacks; one allied creature within 10 ft. may immediately make one weapon attack as a reaction.

> [!info] Stat Block — Celestine, The Operator
> **CR** 3 | **Type** Humanoid | **Role** Support / Tactician
>
> | STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
> |-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
> | 10 (+0) | 15 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 17 (+3) | 14 (+2) | 16 (+3) |
>
> **HP:** 44 | **AC:** 14 (boned corset and composure)
> **Skills:** Insight +6, Investigation +5, Perception +4, Persuasion +5
>
> **Traits:**
> - *The Ledger.* At the start of each of her turns, Celestine may move one creature she can see up to 10 ft. in any direction (no saving throw). She is repositioning. She has been repositioning everything her entire career.
> - *Cold Calculus.* Celestine is immune to the Charmed condition and has advantage on Insight checks. She already knows what you're going to try.
> - *Professional Pride.* Same as Fenwick's. If anything, worse.
>
> **Actions:**
> - *Conductor's Baton.* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 1d6+2 piercing (the baton ends in a thin metal point; it was always a weapon).
> - *Direct the Act (Recharge 5–6).* Celestine points at a creature within 60 ft. Until the end of her next turn, all attacks made against that creature have advantage and it has disadvantage on saving throws.
>
> **Reaction:**
> - *Footnote.* When Celestine would take damage, she may use her reaction to redirect up to half that damage to Fenwick, if he is within 30 ft. and conscious. He takes it without comment.

> [!tip] Running the Ringmasters Together
> Celestine and Fenwick fight as a unit. Keep them roughly 20–30 ft. apart so Celestine benefits from *The Ledger* and Fenwick from *Centre of Attention*. When one drops, the other's behaviour changes:
>
> **If Fenwick falls first:** Celestine stops moving. She looks at him. She looks at the party. She finishes the fight without speaking — not because she has given up, but because there is nothing left to perform for.
>
> **If Celestine falls first:** Fenwick loses his rhythm. His sentences don't get finished. *The Show Must Go On* activates but you can play it as desperation rather than bravado.
>
> **When both are defeated:** They do not flee. They have no exit. They sit in the sawdust together — the natural endpoint of thirty years of partnership, in a ring they have owned for longer than most people the party has met have been alive. Fenwick looks at the ceiling. Celestine closes her eyes.
>
> The seal on the tent dissolves when both are incapacitated.

> [!note] The Interrogation — What They Will Say
> Neither will say the Coachman's name. They know it. They will not say it.
>
> What they will say, if the players push:
> - The children who perform well are *transferred.* To where, they do not say. They do not ask.
> - The Circus has a contract with a company. The wagon wheel is the company's mark.
> - Celestine's ledger contains: names of transferred children, dates of transfer, a contact address (a harbour office, a shipping company name — the Grand Coach Company).
> - There are more operations. The Circus is one of several. Fenwick says this quietly: *"We are not the worst of them."* He does not appear to find comfort in this.

# After the Fight

> [!success] Tigger Comes Home
> When the seal breaks and the tent opens, Tigger is already at the ring's edge, looking at the grounds outside. He does not say anything for a moment. Then he says:
>
> *"The Wood is— is it still there?"*
>
> When told yes: something happens in his face that is not quite bouncing but is the thing that bouncing comes from. He says: *"Right. Right then. We should go. I know some very fast routes. I've been through every part of the area, sort of sideways and quite quickly, but I remember the shapes."*
>
> He looks at his hands once more. Then he takes a running start and bounces — really bounces, full height, full arc, over a wagon and back. He lands badly and doesn't care at all.
>
> *"Still a Tigger,"* he says, to no one in particular.

> [!tip] The Children
> The children performing in the Circus are not imprisoned. They are contracted — agreements signed by desperate or deceived parents. They cannot simply be taken. The party can:
>
> - **Destroy or remove the contract documents** (Celestine's ledger contains the current roster). Without paperwork, the Circus has no enforceable claim.
> - **Free the children directly**, which is possible now that the Ringmasters are incapacitated, but will require dealing with roustabouts and handlers before the Circus regroups.
> - **Alert the town watch**, which is more complex than it sounds; Fenwick has cultivated civic relationships for a long time. A player who succeeds on a DC 14 Persuasion check with the right officials, backed by the ledger as evidence, can get the Circus shut down and the children placed in temporary care.
>
> This does not need to be fully resolved on the night. Some children go home. Some situations are ongoing. That is accurate.

> [!tip] What the Party Learns
> From the interrogation and from Celestine's ledger:
> - **The Circus is a sorting facility.** Children who "perform" go to Pleasure Island. The Circus filters them.
> - **The Grand Coach Company** is the entity receiving the transfers. A harbour address is in the ledger.
> - **The wagon wheel symbol** is the Company's mark. Every operation the party has encountered is connected to the same entity.
> - **Fenwick's admission:** *"There are more operations."* The players may now have enough to connect the Chapter 3 board to what they've seen here.
